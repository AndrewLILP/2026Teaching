Check your Tinkercad design

Before you export anything, make sure your model is ready to print. A small check now saves a wasted print later.

Is your model the right size?

Tinkercad works in millimetres. The Bambu P2S print bed is 256 × 256 × 256 mm. Your model must fit inside that cube.

What you're making Suggested size Status Notes
General small object Under 100 mm in any direction ✓ Safe Prints fast, less waste if something goes wrong
BBC micro:bit slot/holder 52 mm wide × 42 mm deep × 6–12 mm tall ✓ Safe Micro:bit board is 52 × 42 mm. Add 0.3–0.5 mm clearance around edges
Virtual pet shell (micro:bit inside) 60–80 mm wide × 50–65 mm deep × 15–25 mm tall ✓ Ideal Leaves room for the board plus walls at least 1.5 mm thick
Large housing 100–200 mm ⚠ Long print Allow 2–5+ hours. Bed adhesion becomes more important
Over 256 mm in any direction — ✗ Too big Won't fit. Split into two parts and join with screws or glue
📏
Check dimensions in Tinkercad

Click on your shape → look for the W / D / H boxes in the top-right panel. These are in millimetres.

Wall thickness

Walls thinner than 1.2 mm will be fragile or may not print at all. Aim for at least 1.5 mm on any wall that needs to hold something (like a micro:bit).

💡
Intermediate tip

The P2S default nozzle is 0.4 mm. Most slicers use 2–3 perimeters, giving you a wall of 0.8–1.2 mm. Add one more perimeter or increase wall thickness in Tinkercad to stay safe.

⚙️
Developer note

If you need precise tolerances for a snap-fit joint or button cutout, design for 0.2–0.3 mm clearance and always test-print a small coupon first. PLA shrinks slightly on cooling — typically 0.2–0.4% linear.

Export as STL

  1. In Tinkercad, click Export (top right)
  2. Choose .STL — not OBJ or SVG
  3. Save to somewhere easy to find (your Desktop or a USB drive)

STL files are saved as a mesh of triangles. Tinkercad exports clean STLs with no repair needed for basic shapes.

Bambu Studio overview

Bambu Studio is the software that converts your 3D model into instructions the printer can follow. This process is called slicing.

A — Slice Plate

The main button. Click this after your model looks correct. It processes your model into print instructions.

B — Export ▾ menu

Click the small ▾ arrow beside the send button to find Export Plate Sliced File. This is how you save to USB.

C — Your model

Your design appears on the virtual build plate. It should be sitting flat, not floating above it.

D — Print info

After slicing, check the estimated time and filament amount before saving to USB.

E — Supports

Toggle supports on if your model has parts that hang in mid-air. Use Tree type for best results.

F — Print profile

Leave this on the default 0.20mm Quality unless you have a reason to change it.

Developer: Slicing settings worth knowing

  • Layer height: 0.20mm is the sweet spot. 0.10mm = sharper detail, double the time. 0.30mm = faster, slightly rougher.
  • Infill: 15% is fine for most things. Go to 30–40% if parts need to be strong (hinges, clips, micro:bit clips).
  • Walls: 3 perimeters is the default and works well. Bump to 4 for structural parts.
  • Tree supports: Better than normal supports for organic shapes — easier to remove, less scarring.
  • Brim: Add a 5mm brim if the model has a small base footprint and might lift off the plate.

Bambu P2S printer buttons

The P2S has a touchscreen and a few physical buttons. Here's what each one does.

①

Touchscreen

The main control for the printer. Use it to select your file from the USB, start a print, pause, and cancel. Tap your file name to start.

Also used to set filament type, run calibrations, adjust Z-offset, and view live temperature readings.

②

Power button

Hold for 3 seconds to turn on or off. Do not turn off mid-print — always cancel the print from the screen first.

③

USB port

Plug your USB drive in here. The printer reads .3mf files only — not .stl. If the file doesn't appear, see the troubleshooting section.

④

Filament feed

Where the filament spool connects. The printer auto-loads filament when you insert the tube. Use the touchscreen's filament menu to unload or change it.

⑤

Print head

Moves on X and Y axes to lay down each layer. Never touch or push the print head while the printer is on.

The P2S uses a Cartesian XY gantry. The build plate moves on the Z axis. If you hear grinding, the motion system may be obstructed — pause and check.

⑥

Build plate

The surface your model prints on. Remove it carefully after printing — it flexes to release the print. Don't scrape it with metal tools.

Clean with isopropyl alcohol before each print for consistent adhesion. Avoid touching the surface with bare hands — skin oil reduces adhesion.

Advanced guide

Ready to go beyond the basics? These settings give you more control over quality, strength, and print speed.

Supports — when and how

Supports hold up overhanging parts while they print. After the print, you remove them.

Overhang angleSupports needed?What to do
0–45°Usually noPLA can bridge these angles without help
45–60°SometimesTest print a small section first
60–90° (horizontal)YesEnable Tree supports in Bambu Studio
Bridges under 50mmOften noPLA can bridge short gaps well with good cooling
Bridges over 50mmAdd supportsSagging likely otherwise

Orientation — think before you slice

How you orient your model on the plate affects strength, surface quality, and whether you need supports.

  • Print the largest flat surface on the bottom — best adhesion, no supports needed
  • Holes oriented vertically print as circles — horizontally they print as ovals
  • Parts that will be pulled (like a phone holder arm) are strongest when the layers run along the force direction
  • Tall, thin models (columns, towers) can wobble and fail — print them tilted at 15–20° or add a brim

Infill — what's inside

Infill is the internal structure. Higher percentage = stronger, heavier, slower.

  • 10–15% — decorative models, figurines
  • 15–20% — most functional parts, cases, holders
  • 30–40% — clips, hinges, parts under stress
  • Gyroid or Grid pattern — best for even strength in all directions

Troubleshooting

Work through each section in order. Most problems have a simple fix.

USB file not showing on printer
⚠️
This is the most common issue in the classroom — cause not yet confirmed for all cases

Work through these steps in order. Document what worked so we can update this guide.

  1. Remove the USB and reinsert it firmly. Wait 5 seconds. Check if the file appears.
  2. Check the file type — the printer only reads .3mf files. If you saved an .stl, go back to Bambu Studio and use Export Plate Sliced File from the ▾ menu.
  3. Check the file is in the root of the USB (not inside a folder). Move it if needed.
  4. On the printer touchscreen, go to the file list and pull down to refresh.
  5. On a computer, check the USB drive. Is the file actually there? Is it .3mf?
  6. Check the USB format — it should be FAT32. On Windows: right-click drive → Properties → File system. On Mac: Disk Utility → select drive → Get Info. If it says exFAT or NTFS, reformat it to FAT32.
  7. Try a different USB drive. Some drives have timing/compatibility issues with the P2S.
  8. Re-export from Bambu Studio with a simpler filename — no spaces or special characters.
  9. If still failing — tell your teacher. Note which step you reached so the issue can be tracked.
Model is floating above the build plate
  1. Right-click your model in Bambu Studio → select Lay Flat.
  2. If Lay Flat doesn't help, manually rotate the model so the largest flat surface touches the plate.
  3. Check the bottom of the model in Tinkercad — is it actually flat, or is there an unintended curve?
Print not sticking to the build plate (first layer lifting)
  1. Remove the build plate and clean it with a dry cloth or IPA (isopropyl alcohol). Avoid touching the surface with your hands.
  2. Make sure the model is truly flat on the plate (use Lay Flat in Bambu Studio).
  3. Check the first layer is being squished onto the plate — if it looks like a thin thread floating above the surface, the Z-offset may need adjusting. Ask your teacher.
  4. Add a brim in Bambu Studio (Supports → Brim → 5mm). This gives the print more surface area to hold on.
  5. Cancel, let the plate cool fully (5 min), then restart. PLA often releases when cool.
Tangled or twisted filament
🛑
Pause the print first

If you notice the filament is tangled on the spool, pause from the touchscreen before it causes a failed print.

To untangle:
  1. Pause the print via the touchscreen.
  2. Find where the filament is crossing over itself on the spool. Gently lift the top strand over the bottom.
  3. Make sure the filament feeds freely from the spool with no binding.
  4. Resume the print.
Unload and reload filament
ℹ️
Ask your teacher before changing filament

Filament changes should be supervised in a classroom setting.

Unload steps
🔴
1. Go to Settings → Filament → Unload

Use the touchscreen. The printer will heat the nozzle to the correct temperature automatically.

⏳
2. Wait for the nozzle to heat (about 60 seconds)

Do not pull the filament while it's cold. You will damage the extruder.

↑
3. Confirm on screen when prompted

The printer will retract the filament automatically. Guide it gently if it bunches at the tube.

🧵
4. Remove the filament tube from the printhead

Press the grey clip and pull. Wind the filament back onto the spool neatly — loose filament tangles easily.

Reload steps
✂️
1. Cut the end of the filament cleanly at 45°

A clean angle feeds into the tube much more easily than a blunt or frayed end.

→
2. Feed the filament into the tube from the spool

Push it until you feel resistance (the extruder). Don't force it.

📱
3. Go to Settings → Filament → Load

Select your filament type (usually PLA). The printer will heat and then pull it through automatically.

✓
4. Wait for the purge

A small blob of filament will extrude from the nozzle. This clears any old material. Once it runs cleanly in the new colour, you're ready.

Spaghetti print (messy tangle of filament)
🛑
Stop the print immediately

A spaghetti print means the model has detached from the plate. Continuing will make it worse and may jam the nozzle.

  1. Cancel the print from the touchscreen.
  2. Wait for the print head to move to its home position.
  3. Carefully remove the tangled filament — use your fingers, not metal tools.
  4. Check the build plate for any filament still stuck. Clean if needed.
  5. Check the nozzle is clear — heat it via Filament → Load and purge a small amount.
  6. Identify why it happened: was the first layer sticking? Was the model orientated correctly? Did the model have supports where needed?
  7. Fix the issue in Bambu Studio and try again.
Warping (corners lifting off plate)
  1. Clean the build plate with IPA. Even light finger grease causes warping.
  2. Add a brim in Bambu Studio — at least 5mm. This anchors the corners.
  3. Ensure the enclosure door is closed during printing — cold drafts cause warping in PLA.
  4. Check the bed temperature is set correctly for your filament (PLA = 35°C, PETG = 70°C).
Clogged nozzle (filament not coming out)
⚠️
Tell your teacher — don't attempt to fix a clog yourself

Clearing a clog incorrectly can damage the hotend.

Signs of a clog: filament stops extruding mid-print, clicking sound from the extruder, gaps in layers.

For the teacher: perform a cold pull (heat to 200°C, cool to 90°C, pull firmly) or replace the nozzle if repeated clogs occur.

Layer gaps or poor surface quality
  1. Check the filament spool is not tangled and feeds freely.
  2. In Bambu Studio, try increasing the number of walls (Walls & Surface → Wall loops from 3 to 4).
  3. Check the nozzle for partial clog — try a filament purge (Settings → Filament → Load).
  4. If the issue is only on the top surface, increase the number of top layers (default is 4, try 5–6).

Get help

You've tried the troubleshooting steps and it's still not working. That's completely fine — stop here and ask your teacher.

🙋 Stop and ask your teacher

Before you ask, note down what's happening so your teacher can help you faster:

  • What step you're on
  • What you expected to happen
  • What actually happened
  • Which troubleshooting steps you already tried
  • Any error message on the screen

Don't guess with the printer hardware

These things should always involve a teacher:

  • Clogged nozzle
  • Changing the Z-offset calibration
  • Anything involving the hotend or print head
  • Filament change (unless specifically shown how)
  • Printer error codes you don't recognise
