Dice
District

Build a 3D Monopoly-inspired board game in Unity 6. In groups of 2–3, you will design, code, test, and evaluate a working digital board game — earning your place on the leaderboard one sprint at a time.

Unity 6 C# · OOP 10 Hours Groups of 2–3

Project Overview

Your city. Your rules. Your code.

Dice District is a 10-hour collaborative project where you design and build a 3D board game using Unity 6 and C#. Inspired by Monopoly, your game must include a 3D board, working token movement, a property ownership system, and a win condition.

The project runs through all four phases of the Design & Production Framework — from identifying what your game needs to be, through to evaluating it against real player feedback. Your project notebook is your primary evidence of thinking.

"Design and build a 3D Monopoly-inspired board game in Unity 6 for 2–4 local hot-seat players. Your game must include a functioning game loop, property ownership mechanics, and a win condition. Document every decision."

Assessment Connection

Why this project matters

Dice District is a rich learning task — it builds directly toward two upcoming assessments. Every decision you make, every bug you fix, and every reflection you write is exam and portfolio preparation.

Assessment 2 · Exam Preparation

Short-Answer Exam

Working through all four D&P phases under time pressure sharpens your ability to describe design decisions, explain algorithms, and evaluate solutions — exactly what exam questions ask. Your notebook is a revision document.

Assessment 3 · Project Proposal

Major Project Proposal

Dice District is a complete project cycle in miniature. The skills you practise here — defining requirements, planning classes, testing systematically, evaluating honestly — are the skills your proposal must demonstrate you can apply independently.
