CT5-DAT-01

Data and information

Understands how data is represented, transmitted and secured in digital systems and how information is communicated in a range of contexts.

Investigating enterprise Transmitting data Modelling networks

Key content

  • Describe how data is stored, processed and transmitted across connected systems, including binary representation, data types and file formats
  • Investigate how networks enable data transmission, including wired and wireless protocols, hardware components and network topologies
  • Explain how data is secured in transit and at rest using encryption, authentication and access control mechanisms
  • Examine how enterprise information systems use inputs, storage, processes and outputs to serve organisational and social purposes
  • Explore how information is communicated across personal, social and enterprise contexts, considering diverse audiences and ethical responsibilities
CT5-DAT-02

Data analysis

Acquires, represents, analyses and visualises simple and structured data to support decision-making.

Analysing data Designing for user experience

Key content

  • Define a real-world problem or question solvable through data analysis, identifying users, stakeholders and the scope of the solution
  • Collect and organise data using databases, spreadsheets and structured queries, selecting appropriate data types and structures
  • Identify patterns, trends and outliers in datasets using statistical analysis, filtering, sorting and aggregation
  • Generate data visualisations — charts, graphs, maps and dashboards — to communicate findings clearly for a target audience
  • Analyse data to make informed decisions and produce reports that directly address the original problem or question
CT5-SAF-01

Safety, security and ethics

Applies safe, secure and ethical practices when using and managing data and information in digital systems.

Transmitting data Modelling networks Analysing data

Key content

  • Apply cybersecurity principles — including encryption, firewalls, two-factor authentication and access control — to protect data in enterprise systems
  • Investigate legal and ethical responsibilities when collecting, storing and sharing data, including privacy legislation and data minimisation principles
  • Evaluate privacy implications and vulnerabilities of enterprise information systems, identifying risks to individuals, organisations and communities
  • Critically examine the social impact of data collection and algorithmic systems on diverse groups, including Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples
  • Apply copyright, consent and ethical use principles when sourcing, publishing and communicating data and digital content
CT5-COM-01

Communicating with ICT

Selects and uses appropriate digital tools to communicate and collaborate effectively across a range of contexts and audiences.

Analysing data Designing for user experience

Key content

  • Select appropriate formats, tools and platforms to present data findings clearly to specific target audiences and contexts
  • Create multimodal reports and presentations combining data visualisations, written analysis and interactive or dynamic elements
  • Apply communication design principles — including accessibility, clarity and visual hierarchy — across digital outputs and dashboards
  • Use collaborative digital tools to plan, assign, share and review project work with peers and stakeholders throughout the project lifecycle
  • Communicate social and ethical dimensions of data findings, supporting claims with evidence and acknowledging limitations
CT5-DES-01

Design qualities

Applies design qualities and principles to produce fit-for-purpose digital solutions that meet user and contextual needs.

Analysing data Designing for user experience

Key content

  • Apply design qualities — functionality, accessibility, usability and aesthetics — to enterprise information solutions, dashboards and user interfaces
  • Evaluate designs against user needs and requirements, considering diverse groups including people with disability and varied cultural backgrounds
  • Adhere to privacy, copyright and legal requirements throughout design and production, documenting compliance decisions
  • Document design decisions and iterations using wireframes, mockups, flowcharts and written rationale across each design phase
  • Refine designs in response to user feedback and testing, applying iterative cycles of prototyping and evaluation
CT5-EVL-01

Testing and evaluation

Tests and evaluates solutions against predetermined functional and non-functional requirements, considering social, ethical and cybersecurity dimensions.

All content areas

Key content

  • Define functional and non-functional requirements and establish clear success criteria before building or analysing enterprise solutions
  • Test solutions systematically — including data accuracy, system security and interface usability — documenting results and anomalies
  • Evaluate solutions against social, ethical, legal and cybersecurity requirements, identifying strengths, gaps and improvements
  • Communicate evaluation findings using data, visualisations, written analysis and honest reflection on what could be improved
  • Assess the efficiency and effectiveness of network or data system solutions against real-world operational and user constraints
