What this arc is about

Verification by substitution is how mathematicians check their work. You put your answer back into the original equation and show that both sides are equal. If they're not — the answer is wrong, regardless of how confident you felt.

You'll also use substitution the other way: starting with a formula, substituting known values to create an equation, then solving it. This is how real-world problems are solved in science, engineering, and finance.

Verification by substitution

To verify a solution, substitute the value back into the original equation and check whether LHS = RHS. If they match, the solution is proved correct. Always verify after solving — it catches arithmetic errors.

Equation to verify
4n − 3 = 17,   proposed: n = 5
Substitute n = 5 into the LHS
LHS = 4(5) − 3 = 20 − 3 = 17
Compare to RHS
LHS = 17 = 17 = RHS   ✓
Conclusion
n = 5 is a verified solution to 4n − 3 = 17.
Quick check
Is x = 3 a solution to 5x − 1 = 14?

Formulas — substituting to create an equation

A formula is a general equation linking several variables. When you substitute known values into a formula, you create a new equation with one unknown — which you can then solve.

Formula A = l × w  (area of a rectangle)
Known values
A = 56 m²,   l = 8 m,   find w
Substitute known values
56 = 8 × w
The formula becomes a one-step linear equation
Solve (divide both sides by 8)
w = 7 m
Verify by substitution
Check: A = 8 × 7 = 56 m² ✓
Quick check
Using P = 2l + 2w with P = 40 and l = 12, what equation do you get after substituting?

Formulas with division — reading them correctly

Some formulas involve division. When written with a fraction bar, the numerator is divided by the denominator. Substitute known values, then solve the resulting equation.

Formula v = dt  (speed, distance, time)
Known values
d = 420 km,   t = 6 h,   find v
Substitute into the formula
v = 4206
Evaluate the division
v = 70 km/h
Verify
Check: d = v × t = 70 × 6 = 420 km ✓
Quick check
Using v = d/t with d = 300 and v = 60, what equation do you get? What is t?
Practise — Verification & Formulas

Two sections: check proposed solutions by substitution, then apply formulas to real-world contexts.

For each equation below, a solution has been proposed. Verify by substitution — then decide: correct or incorrect?

5x − 4 = 11
Proposed solution: x = 3
Substitute x = 3:   LHS = 5(3) − 4 = 15 − 4 = 11   |   RHS = 11
LHS = RHS ✓   x = 3 is a valid solution.
3m + 2 = 20
Proposed solution: m = 5
Substitute m = 5:   LHS = 3(5) + 2 = 15 + 2 = 17   |   RHS = 20
LHS ≠ RHS ✗   m = 5 is NOT a valid solution. (Correct answer: m = 6)
2n − 7 = 9
Proposed solution: n = 7
Substitute n = 7:   LHS = 2(7) − 7 = 14 − 7 = 7   |   RHS = 9
LHS ≠ RHS ✗   n = 7 is NOT a valid solution. (Correct answer: n = 8)
p4 + 1 = 4
Proposed solution: p = 12
Substitute p = 12:   LHS = 12/4 + 1 = 3 + 1 = 4   |   RHS = 4
LHS = RHS ✓   p = 12 is a valid solution.

Each problem gives you a formula and some known values. Substitute, solve, and verify.

A rectangular swimming pool has an area of 72 m². Its length is 9 m. What is its width?
Formula A = l × w
Substitute known values to create an equation:
72 = 9 × w
w = m
Divide both sides by 9: 72 ÷ 9 = w
A rectangular paddock has a perimeter of 64 m and a length of 20 m. Find the width.
Formula P = 2l + 2w
Substitute known values:
64 = 2(20) + 2w   →   64 = 40 + 2w
w = m
Step 1: Subtract 40 from both sides → 2w = 24. Step 2: Divide by 2 → w = 12.
A train travels 480 km in 6 hours at a constant speed. What is the speed of the train?
Formula v = dt
Substitute known values:
v = 4806
v = km/h
Divide 480 by 6. This is a direct substitution — no further solving needed.
A casual worker earned $168 at a rate of $21 per hour. How many hours did they work?
Formula P = r × h
Substitute known values:
168 = 21 × h
h = hours
Divide both sides by 21: 168 ÷ 21 = h
General strategy — Hint Ladder
For verification: what does it mean for a solution to be "correct"? For formulas: which variable is unknown and which are given?
Verification: substitute the proposed value for every instance of the pronumeral, evaluate LHS, compare to RHS.
Formulas: write the formula, substitute known values, identify the resulting equation type (one-step or two-step), then solve.
Verification example: Is x = 5 a solution to 3x − 4 = 11?
LHS = 3(5) − 4 = 15 − 4 = 11. RHS = 11. LHS = RHS ✓

Formula example: A = l × w, A = 35, l = 7, find w.
Substitute: 35 = 7 × w → Divide by 7 → w = 5. Check: 7 × 5 = 35 ✓
