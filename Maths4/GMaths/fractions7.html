Addition and Subtraction of Fractions

1. Key Words

  • Denominator: The bottom number; the total parts a whole is divided into.
  • Numerator: The top number; the number of parts we have.
  • Common Denominator: A shared denominator needed to add or subtract fractions.
  • Mixed Number: A whole number combined with a fraction (e.g., 1 ½).

2. Concepts

To add or subtract fractions, they must have the same denominator. If they do not, use equivalent fractions to find a common denominator.

Same denominator: a⁄c + b⁄c = (a+b)⁄c

For whole numbers: Treat the whole number as a fraction (e.g., 2 = 6⁄3).

3. Visual Representation

Imagine a fraction wall where 1/2 is equal to 3/6 and 1/3 is equal to 2/6. To add them, you align them to the 1/6 pieces.

4. Worked Examples

Example: Calculate 2⁄3 + 1⁄4

Step 1: Common denominator is 12 (3 × 4).

Step 2: Convert 2⁄3 = 8⁄12 and 1⁄4 = 3⁄12.

Step 3: Add the numerators: 8 + 3 = 11. Answer: 11⁄12.

5. Error Detective

Common Mistake: 1⁄2 + 1⁄3 = 2⁄5 (Adding denominators).

Correction: Denominators must be the same! Convert to 3⁄6 + 2⁄6 = 5⁄6.

6. Word Problems

Challenge: A recipe needs 1 ½ cups of flour. If you only have a 1⁄4 cup measuring scoop, how many scoops do you need?
