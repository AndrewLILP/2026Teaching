⚖ You've found something important!
When both sides of a scale are equal, we say it is balanced. Mathematicians write this as an equation — a number sentence with an equals sign (=) showing both sides have the same value.

For example, if you place 5 on the left and 3 + 2 on the right: 5 = 3 + 2 That's an equation. The = sign is the claim that both sides balance.
1
What is a pronumeral?

A pronumeral is a letter that stands in for an unknown or changing number. Instead of writing "some number", we write a letter like x, n, or m.

In words
"A number plus 3 equals 8"
Hard to work with mathematically
With a pronumeral
n + 3 = 8
Clear and precise — we can solve it!

The letter n is the pronumeral — it holds the place of the unknown number until we figure out what it is. Any letter works; x is most common, but the choice is just a convention.

Number sentences and equations

An equation is a number sentence that contains an equals sign (=). The equals sign means "has the same value as" — exactly like the balance scale being level.

7 + 3 = 10

Read this as: "Seven plus three has the same value as ten." Replace any number with a pronumeral and you have an equation with an unknown:

7 + n = 10

Now n is unknown. The equation claims that some number added to 7 gives 10. That's something we can solve.

Expressions vs equations — the crucial difference

This is one of the most common sources of confusion in algebra. Here's the distinction:

Expression
3x + 2
A mathematical phrase — no equals sign, no claim
Equation
3x + 2 = 14
A mathematical sentence — makes a claim using =

Think of it this way: an expression is like a noun phrase ("the red car"). An equation is like a full sentence making a claim ("the red car is parked outside"). The equals sign is what turns a phrase into a claim you can test — and solve.
