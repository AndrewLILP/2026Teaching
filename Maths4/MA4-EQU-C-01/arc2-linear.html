Inverse operations — undoing what was done

Every operation has an inverse — an operation that undoes it. To isolate the unknown, we apply the inverse operation to both sides. This keeps the equation balanced, like always adding equal weights to both pans.

+  ↔  −
Addition and subtraction undo each other
×  ↔  ÷
Multiplication and division undo each other

The key rule: whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other. This is the balance-scale principle from Arc 1.

Solving one-step equations

A one-step equation needs exactly one operation to isolate the pronumeral. Identify what's being done to the unknown, then apply the inverse to both sides.

Given equation
x + 7 = 15
x has 7 added to it
Step 1 — Subtract 7 from both sides (inverse of +7)
x + 7 − 7 = 15 − 7
The +7 and −7 cancel out
Simplified
x = 8
Verify by substitution — always check your answer!
Substitute x = 8 into the original equation:
LHS = 8 + 7 = 15
RHS = 15
LHS = RHS ✓   The solution is correct.

Solving two-step equations — unwrap from the outside in

A two-step equation has two operations applied to the unknown. Think of it like a parcel wrapped in two layers — you unwrap from the outside first. Undo the addition or subtraction before the multiplication or division.

Given equation
2x + 3 = 11
x is multiplied by 2, then 3 is added
Step 1 — Undo the addition: subtract 3 from both sides
2x + 3 − 3 = 11 − 3
Simplified after step 1
2x = 8
Step 2 — Undo the multiplication: divide both sides by 2
2x ÷ 2 = 8 ÷ 2
Solution
x = 4
Verify by substitution
Substitute x = 4:   LHS = 2(4) + 3 = 8 + 3 = 11
RHS = 11   →   LHS = RHS ✓

General strategy — the Hint Ladder
Look at the pronumeral. What operations have been applied to it? List them — that's what you need to undo.
Work backwards through the operations. If the equation has ax + b = c: first undo the +b (subtract b), then undo the ×a (divide by a). Always do addition/subtraction before multiplication/division.
Example:   5y − 3 = 17
Step 1: Add 3 to both sides → 5y = 20
Step 2: Divide both sides by 5 → y = 4
Verify: 5(4) − 3 = 17 ✓
