What is an equation?

An equation is like a set of scales — both sides must balance. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other side. The goal is to get x alone.

x + 5
12

1-Step Equations

One operation separates x from its answer. Undo it by doing the opposite:

+ → − − → + × → ÷ ÷ → ×

Worked Example:   x + 5 = 12

1
5 is added to x. The opposite of adding is subtracting. Subtract 5 from both sides.
x + 5 − 5 = 12 − 5
2
Simplify both sides.
x = 7

2-Step Equations

Two operations applied to x — undo them in reverse order (BODMAS backwards). Usually: undo the + or − first, then the × or ÷.

Worked Example:   3x + 4 = 19

1
Subtract 4 from both sides (undo the addition first).
3x = 15
2
Divide both sides by 3 (undo the multiplication).
x = 5
