Ratios 🏎️

Mario Kart's item system is a masterpiece of ratio design. Let's find out how the maths works — and why it makes the game fair and fun at the same time.

🎮 Game Designer's Lens

In Mario Kart, item boxes don't give every item equally often. Designers use ratios to control how often each item appears — making sure players in last place get better items than the race leader. This balance is called rubber-banding, and ratios are how it works.

📚 What is a Ratio?
RATIO
Definition
  • Compares two or more quantities in the same units
  • Written as a : b or a : b : c
  • Order matters — 3 : 1 ≠ 1 : 3
Examples
  • Stars : Bananas = 3 : 12
  • Red : Blue karts = 2 : 5
  • Cement : Sand = 1 : 3
Non-examples
  • 3 km/h (this is a RATE — different units)
  • 50% (this is a percentage)
  • 0.25 (this is a decimal)
Game connection
  • Item frequencies in loot boxes
  • Kart stat distributions (speed : handling)
  • Team compositions in strategy games
✏️ Worked Example 1 — Simplifying a Ratio
🏎️ A Mario Kart character has a Speed : Handling stat ratio of 15 : 10. Write this in simplest form.
Step 1

Find the HCF (highest common factor) of 15 and 10.

Factors of 15: 1, 3, 5, 15
Factors of 10: 1, 2, 5, 10
HCF = 5
Step 2

Divide both parts of the ratio by the HCF.

15 ÷ 5 = 3    10 ÷ 5 = 2
Step 3

Write the simplified ratio. Check: can 3 and 2 be divided by anything? No — we're done.

Speed : Handling = 3 : 2 ✓
✏️ Worked Example 2 — Part as a Fraction
🎁 In our item box, Stars : Red Shells : Bananas : Mushrooms = 3 : 9 : 12 : 6. What fraction of all items are Mushrooms?
Step 1

Find the total number of parts: add all ratio values.

3 + 9 + 12 + 6 = 30 parts total
Step 2

Write the Mushroom part as a fraction of the total.

Mushroom fraction = 6/30
Step 3

Simplify the fraction (HCF of 6 and 30 is 6).

6/30 = 1/5

So Mushrooms make up 1/5 of all items.

✏️ Worked Example 3 — Dividing a Quantity in a Ratio
🏆 Two players win a team tournament prize of $150. They agree to split it in the ratio 2 : 3 based on how many races they won. How much does each player get?
Step 1

Count the total number of parts in the ratio.

2 + 3 = 5 parts
Step 2

Find the value of ONE part using the unitary method.

$150 ÷ 5 = $30 per part
Step 3

Multiply to find each player's share.

Player A: 2 × $30 = $60
Player B: 3 × $30 = $90
Check

Verify the shares add back to the total.

$60 + $90 = $150 ✓
🔍 Error Detective

A student wrote this solution. Find the mistake and explain what they should have done instead.

Question: Simplify the ratio 18 : 24.

Student's working:
18 : 24
Divide both by 2: 9 : 12
Divide both by 2: 4.5 : 6
Answer: 4.5 : 6

What went wrong? Write your explanation below, then reveal the answer.


The error: The student divided by 2 a second time when 9 and 12 were no longer divisible by 2 evenly — this created a decimal ratio, which is incorrect. Ratios should use whole numbers.

The fix: After 9 : 12, find the HCF of 9 and 12, which is 3. Divide both by 3: 9 ÷ 3 = 3 and 12 ÷ 3 = 4. Correct answer: 3 : 4. Or, work smarter: HCF of 18 and 24 is 6. Divide in one step: 18 ÷ 6 = 3, 24 ÷ 6 = 4.
