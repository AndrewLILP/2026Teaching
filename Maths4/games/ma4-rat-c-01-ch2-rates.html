Rates 🏁

Racing simulators like Forza and Gran Turismo are built on rates — speed, fuel burn, tyre wear. Understand rates and you understand how these games model the real world.

🎮 Game Designer's Lens

Every stat on a car's data sheet in Forza or Gran Turismo is a rate. Top speed is measured in km/h. Fuel efficiency is litres per 100 km. Tyre wear is percentage lost per lap. Game engineers convert real-world measurements into these rates to make the simulation feel authentic. Today we'll learn the maths they use.

🔍 Ratio or Rate?

A key idea in this chapter is the difference between a ratio and a rate. Look at each item and decide — is it a ratio or a rate?

1. A car travels 300 km using 25 litres of fuel. The comparison 300 : 25 is...
2. In a racing game, two cars have tyre ratings of 85 and 65 (both out of 100). The comparison 85 : 65 is...
3. A pit stop crew changes a tyre in 3.8 seconds. This is best described as...
📚 Ratios vs Rates — Key Differences
Feature Ratio Rate
Units ✓ Same units (or no units) Different units
Written as a : b a per b (e.g. km/h, $/kg)
Example Speed : Handling = 3 : 2 Speed = 120 km/h
Simplified by Dividing both by HCF Making denominator = 1 (unit rate)
Racing game example Red cars : Blue cars = 4 : 3 Fuel use = 8.5 L/100 km
Key idea: A rate always has TWO different types of units connected by "per". Speed is kilometres per hour. Price is dollars per kilogram. Tyre wear is percent per lap.
✏️ Worked Example 1 — Finding a Unit Rate
🏎️ A race car travels 360 km in 3 hours. Express this as a unit rate in km/h.
Step 1

Identify the two quantities and their units.

Distance = 360 km    Time = 3 hours
Step 2

Write as a rate fraction, then divide to make the denominator = 1.

360 km ÷ 3 h = 120 km per hour
Step 3

Write using correct notation.

Speed = 120 km/h ✓

Unit rate = rate per ONE unit. Here: per ONE hour.

✏️ Worked Example 2 — Converting Rate Units
⚡ A car's top speed is listed as 216 km/h. Convert this to metres per second (m/s).
Step 1

Convert kilometres to metres: multiply by 1000.

216 km = 216 × 1000 = 216 000 m
Step 2

Convert hours to seconds: multiply by 3600.

1 hour = 60 × 60 = 3600 seconds
Step 3

Divide distance (m) by time (s).

216 000 ÷ 3600 = 60 m/s ✓

So 216 km/h = 60 m/s. Game engines often use m/s internally!

✏️ Worked Example 3 — Best Buy (Financial Rates)
🎮 An online game store sells gaming credits:
  Pack A: 500 credits for $8.00
  Pack B: 1200 credits for $18.00
Which is the better value?
Step 1

Find the unit rate (price per 100 credits) for each pack.

Pack A: $8.00 ÷ 500 × 100 = $1.60 per 100 credits
Step 2
Pack B: $18.00 ÷ 1200 × 100 = $1.50 per 100 credits
Step 3

Compare: smaller price per unit = better value.

$1.50 < $1.60 → Pack B is better value ✓
🔍 Error Detective

A student tried to convert 90 km/h to m/s. Find the mistake.

Question: Convert 90 km/h to m/s.

Student's working:
90 km/h
× 1000 to convert km → m: 90 000
÷ 60 to convert hours → seconds: 1500
Answer: 1500 m/s

The error: The student divided by 60 instead of 3600 when converting hours to seconds. There are 60 minutes in an hour AND 60 seconds in a minute — so 1 hour = 60 × 60 = 3600 seconds.

Correct working: 90 × 1000 = 90 000 m. Then 90 000 ÷ 3600 = 25 m/s.
