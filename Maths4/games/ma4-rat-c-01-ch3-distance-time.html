Distance–Time Graphs 🗺️

Follow Link's journey across Hyrule. Every movement — fast, slow, stopped, returning — can be drawn as a graph. Learn to read and create these stories in data.

🎮 Game Designer's Lens

In open-world games like The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild, every NPC (non-player character) follows a movement script. Game designers use position-over-time data to program these paths. A village merchant might walk to the market, wait, return home — each action is a segment on a distance–time graph. Understanding these graphs lets you read — and eventually design — movement in any game world.

📚 Reading Distance–Time Graphs
↗ Positive slope = moving away from start → Zero slope = stopped ↘ Negative slope = returning toward start
Key formula: Speed = Distance ÷ Time
On a distance–time graph, speed = the gradient (steepness) of the line segment.
Steeper slope = faster speed.

Each straight-line segment on a distance–time graph represents constant speed. Curved segments would mean changing speed — we focus on straight-line segments in Stage 4.

✏️ Worked Example 1 — Calculating Speed from a Graph
🗡️ Link travels from 20 km to 40 km between hours 4 and 6 (Segment C). Calculate his speed for this segment.
Step 1

Find the distance travelled in this segment.

Distance = 40 km − 20 km = 20 km
Step 2

Find the time taken for this segment.

Time = 6 h − 4 h = 2 hours
Step 3

Apply the formula: Speed = Distance ÷ Time.

Speed = 20 ÷ 2 = 10 km/h ✓
✏️ Worked Example 2 — Speed on the Return Journey
🗺️ In Segment E, Link travels from 50 km back to 20 km between hours 7 and 10. What is his speed on the return journey?
Step 1

Find the distance travelled — use the actual distance covered, not the change in position.

Distance = 50 km − 20 km = 30 km

Note: even though he's returning, the distance COVERED is still 30 km (positive).

Step 2

Find the time for this segment.

Time = 10 − 7 = 3 hours
Step 3

Calculate speed.

Speed = 30 ÷ 3 = 10 km/h ✓

The return journey happens to be the same speed as the outward journey in Segment A.

✏️ Worked Example 3 — Reading a Description and Drawing Segments
🏰 A Zelda NPC guard starts at the village gate (0 km). He walks 6 km in 1 hour, waits for 2 hours, then returns home in 1.5 hours. Describe each segment of his distance–time graph.
Segment 1

Walks 6 km in 1 hour → positive slope.

From (0, 0) to (1, 6)
Speed = 6 ÷ 1 = 6 km/h
Segment 2

Waits for 2 hours → flat, horizontal line.

From (1, 6) to (3, 6)
Speed = 0 km/h
Segment 3

Returns 6 km home in 1.5 hours → negative slope.

From (3, 6) to (4.5, 0)
Speed = 6 ÷ 1.5 = 4 km/h

🔍 Error Detective

A student interpreted this segment: "From hour 3 to hour 7, the character moved from 40 km to 10 km." Find the error in their working.

Student's working:
Distance = 40 − 10 = 30 km
Time = 7 − 3 = 4 hours
Speed = 30 ÷ 4 = 7.5 km/h
Direction: moving away from start
Conclusion: The character moved away at 7.5 km/h.

The error: The speed calculation is correct (7.5 km/h), but the student said the character moved "away from start" when the distance decreased from 40 km to 10 km. Decreasing distance from home means the character is RETURNING toward the start — a negative slope.

Correct conclusion: The character is returning toward the start at 7.5 km/h (negative slope, getting closer to home).
