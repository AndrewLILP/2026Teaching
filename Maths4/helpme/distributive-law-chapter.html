The Distributive Law

Grouping symbols, expanding brackets, and simplifying algebraic expressions

Stage 4 — Years 7–8

📚 Vocabulary — tap a card to flip

The distributive law is one of the most used tools in all of algebra. Getting the vocabulary right first means you will understand exactly what you are doing and why it works — not just following a procedure.

Grouping Symbols

DefinitionSymbols that group terms together and indicate those terms are treated as a single quantity. In algebra, usually parentheses ( ).
Examples3(x + 4): the brackets group x + 4;   −2(a − 5): brackets group a − 5
Non-examples3 × x + 4 (no grouping — only x is multiplied by 3, not x + 4)
Key ideaBrackets tell you: multiply the WHOLE expression inside, not just the first term.

Multiply the outside by every term inside

Distributive Law

DefinitionA property that states: a(b + c) = ab + ac. The factor outside the brackets is distributed (multiplied) to every term inside.
Examples3(x + 4) = 3x + 12;   5(2a − 3) = 10a − 15
Non-examples3(x + 4) ≠ 3x + 4 (only distributed to first term)
Key ideaDistribute = share out. Every term inside gets multiplied by the outside factor.

Expand

DefinitionTo rewrite an expression by removing grouping symbols, using the distributive law. Expanding increases the number of terms.
ExamplesExpand 4(x − 2): → 4x − 8;   Expand 2(3a + 1): → 6a + 2
Non-examplesCollecting like terms (4x + 2x = 6x) is simplifying, not expanding — it reduces terms.
Key ideaExpanding removes the brackets. The result has more visible terms than the original.

Factor (outside)

DefinitionThe number, pronumeral, or term written immediately before the bracket. It multiplies every term inside the bracket when expanded.
ExamplesIn 5(x + 3): factor is 5. In −2(a − 4): factor is −2. In x(x + 1): factor is x.
Non-examplesThe terms inside the brackets are not the factor; they are the terms being multiplied.
Key ideaIf no number is written before the bracket — e.g. (x + 3) — the invisible factor is 1. For −(x + 3), it is −1.

Equivalent Expressions

DefinitionTwo expressions that give the same numerical result for every value of the pronumeral. Expanding produces an equivalent expression.
Examples3(x + 4) and 3x + 12 are equivalent — substituting any x gives the same answer for both.
Non-examples3x + 4 and 3x + 12 are NOT equivalent — they give different values for the same x.
Key ideaExpanding changes the form but NOT the value. You can verify equivalence by substitution.

Expand and Simplify

DefinitionA two-stage process: first apply the distributive law to remove brackets, then collect any like terms that result.
Examples2(x + 3) + 4x → 2x + 6 + 4x → 6x + 6
Non-examplesCollecting like terms before expanding the bracket — brackets must be removed first.
Key ideaOrder matters: expand ALL brackets first, then collect like terms. Never collect before expanding.

A — What do grouping symbols do?

Without brackets: 3 × x + 4

The multiplication only applies to x. The 4 is added afterwards, outside the multiplication.

3 × x + 4  →  3x + 4

Order of operations: × before +. The 4 is not multiplied by 3.

With brackets: 3 × (x + 4)

The brackets force the entire expression (x + 4) to be treated as one quantity — all of it is multiplied by 3.

3 × ( x + 4 )  →  3x + 12

Both x AND 4 are multiplied by 3. The results are different.

⚠️ Key insight: 3(x + 4) ≠ 3x + 4. The bracket means you must multiply 3 by every term inside — including the 4. This is the most common error in expanding.

B — The Distributive Law

General form:   a(b + c) = ab + ac

The outside factor a is distributed — multiplied into every term inside the brackets.

Number example:   3 × (4 + 5) = 3 × 4 + 3 × 5 = 12 + 15 = 27
Check:   3 × (4 + 5) = 3 × 9 = 27   ✓   (same answer)
Algebraic form:   3(x + 4) = 3x + 3×4 = 3x + 12

C — Negative factors require care with signs

Positive factor: easy signs

4(x + 3) = 4x + 12
4(x − 3) = 4x − 12

The sign inside the bracket is preserved — positive stays positive, negative stays negative.

Negative factor: signs flip

−3(x + 2) = −3x − 6
−3(x − 2) = −3x + 6

Negative × positive = negative. Negative × negative = positive. The second sign always flips.

⚠️ The negative-outside trap: −3(x − 2). Both multiplications involve negatives: −3 × x = −3x and −3 × (−2) = +6. The most common error is writing −3x − 6 instead of −3x + 6.

📌 The distributive law — three rules to remember

  • Multiply every term inside the bracket — missing even one term is an error.
  • The sign of each product follows multiplication rules: positive × positive = positive; negative × positive = negative; negative × negative = positive.
  • Expanding is equivalent: 3(x + 2) and 3x + 6 give the same value for every value of x. You can always verify by substituting a number.
  • Invisible coefficient: if no number appears before the bracket — e.g. (x + 4) — the factor is 1. If it is −(x + 4), the factor is −1, giving −x − 4.

✏️ Worked Examples — reveal one step at a time

Example 1 — Basic Expansion

Foundation
Expand  3(x + 4)
Step 1 — Identify the factor and the terms inside
Factor (outside): 3  |  Terms inside: x and +4
The factor 3 sits directly before the bracket. The bracket contains two terms separated by the + sign. Both must be multiplied by 3.
Step 2 — Multiply the factor by the first term
3 × x = 3x
Positive × positive = positive. 3 × x is written as 3x using algebraic convention (no × sign).
Step 3 — Multiply the factor by the second term
3 × 4 = 12
The second term is +4 (positive). 3 × 4 = 12. The sign of the second term in the answer matches the sign inside the bracket.
Step 4 — Write the expanded expression
3(x + 4) = 3x + 12
Join both products: 3x + 12. The brackets have been removed. There are no like terms, so this is fully simplified.
✅ 3(x + 4) = 3x + 12. Verify: let x = 2. Left side: 3(2 + 4) = 3 × 6 = 18. Right side: 3(2) + 12 = 6 + 12 = 18 ✓

Example 2 — Coefficient Inside the Bracket

Foundation
Expand  5(2a − 3)
Step 1 — Identify the factor and terms inside
Factor: 5  |  Terms inside: 2a and −3
The first term inside is 2a (a pronumeral term with coefficient 2). The second term is −3 (negative constant). Both get multiplied by 5.
Step 2 — Multiply 5 by 2a
5 × 2a = 10a
Multiply the coefficients: 5 × 2 = 10. The pronumeral a is unchanged. Result: 10a.
Step 3 — Multiply 5 by −3
5 × (−3) = −15
Positive × negative = negative. 5 × 3 = 15, and the sign stays negative: −15.
Step 4 — Write the expanded expression
5(2a − 3) = 10a − 15
Combine both products. No like terms — the expression is fully simplified.
✅ 5(2a − 3) = 10a − 15

Example 3 — Expand and Simplify

Core
Expand and simplify  2(x + 3) + 4x
Step 1 — Expand the bracket first
2(x + 3) = 2x + 6
Apply the distributive law: 2 × x = 2x and 2 × 3 = 6. Expand before collecting any like terms — never collect first.
Step 2 — Rewrite the full expression without the bracket
2x + 6 + 4x
Replace 2(x + 3) with its expansion 2x + 6. The +4x that was already there follows at the end.
Step 3 — Identify and collect like terms
x-terms: 2x + 4x = 6x  |  constant: 6
2x and 4x are like terms (both have pronumeral x). Add the coefficients: 2 + 4 = 6. The constant 6 has no like partner.
Step 4 — Write the simplified result
2(x + 3) + 4x = 6x + 6
The final expression has two unlike terms. No further simplification is possible.
✅ 2(x + 3) + 4x = 6x + 6. Remember: expand first, then collect.

Example 4 — Negative Factor

Core
Expand  −3(a − 2)
Step 1 — Identify the factor and terms inside
Factor: −3  |  Terms inside: a and −2
The factor is −3 (negative). There are two terms inside: +a (positive) and −2 (negative). Both receive the factor, including its negative sign.
Step 2 — Multiply −3 by a
(−3) × a = −3a
Negative × positive = negative. The first product is −3a.
Step 3 — Multiply −3 by −2 (watch the signs)
(−3) × (−2) = +6
Negative × negative = positive. This is the step most students get wrong — when the factor is negative and the term inside is also negative, the product is positive.
Step 4 — Write the expanded expression
−3(a − 2) = −3a + 6
Combine both products. The second term becomes +6, not −6. Common error: writing −3a − 6 by "keeping" the minus sign instead of multiplying it.
✅ −3(a − 2) = −3a + 6. Verify: let a = 1. Left: −3(1 − 2) = −3(−1) = 3. Right: −3(1) + 6 = −3 + 6 = 3 ✓

Example 5 — Double Expansion and Simplify

Extension
Expand and simplify  2(3x + 1) + 3(x − 4)
Step 1 — Expand the first bracket: 2(3x + 1)
2 × 3x = 6x  |  2 × 1 = 2   →   6x + 2
Distribute 2 to both terms inside the first bracket. 2 × 3x = 6x (multiply coefficients: 2 × 3 = 6) and 2 × 1 = 2.
Step 2 — Expand the second bracket: 3(x − 4)
3 × x = 3x  |  3 × (−4) = −12   →   3x − 12
Distribute 3 to both terms inside the second bracket. Positive × negative = negative: 3 × (−4) = −12.
Step 3 — Rewrite with all brackets removed
6x + 2 + 3x − 12
Replace each bracket with its expansion. The full expression is now bracket-free. Now collect like terms.
Step 4 — Collect like terms and simplify
x-terms: 6x + 3x = 9x
constants: 2 − 12 = −10
Result: 9x − 10
Group the x-terms and constants separately. 6 + 3 = 9 for the x-coefficient. 2 + (−12) = 2 − 12 = −10 for the constant.
✅ 2(3x + 1) + 3(x − 4) = 9x − 10. Always expand both brackets before collecting any like terms.

🔍 Error Detective — find the mistake

🔍 Case 1 — Expanding a bracket
Task: Expand   4(x + 3)

Click the line that contains the error:

Line 1:   Factor outside the bracket: 4  |  Terms inside: x and +3
Line 2:   Multiply 4 by x:   4 × x = 4x
Line 3:   The +3 is already a number, so it stays: keep +3
Line 4:   Answer: 4x + 3
❌ The error is on Line 3

The working correctly distributes to the first term (x) but then fails to multiply the second term (+3) by the factor 4. The reason given — "it is already a number" — is false. Every term inside the bracket must be multiplied by the outside factor, regardless of whether it is a number or a pronumeral.

  • Multiply 4 by x → 4x ✓
  • Multiply 4 by 3 → 12 (not 3)
✅ Correct:   4(x + 3) = 4x + 12

Error pattern: distributing to the first term only and leaving the second term unchanged — the single most common expanding error.

🔍 Case 2 — Negative factor
Task: Expand   −2(a − 5)

Click the line that contains the error:

Line 1:   Factor: −2  |  Terms inside: a and −5
Line 2:   (−2) × a = −2a
Line 3:   (−2) × (−5) = −10   (negative times negative)
Line 4:   Answer: −2a − 10
❌ The error is on Line 3

The multiplication (−2) × (−5) gives a positive result, not a negative one. The working states the answer is −10, which applies the rule negative × positive = negative — but both factors here are negative.

  • Negative × negative = positive
  • (−2) × (−5) = +10
  • Line 4 follows from Line 3's error and is therefore also wrong
✅ Correct:
(−2) × a = −2a
(−2) × (−5) = +10
−2(a − 5) = −2a + 10

Error pattern: treating negative × negative as negative — the most common sign error in expanding brackets.

📖 Word Problems — Newman's Framework

Each problem requires writing a bracketed expression from a real-world situation, then expanding it. Work through the Newman's steps before viewing the solution.

Problem 1 — Rectangle Area

1-step
A rectangle has a width of 4 cm and a length of (x + 3) cm. Write an expression for the area of the rectangle, then expand it. Evaluate the area when x = 5.
① READ
I need to write the _____________ formula for a rectangle, then _____________ the expression, then _____________ when x = 5.
② DEFINE
Width = _____ cm;   Length = (_____ + _____) cm;   x = an unknown length in cm
③ WRITE — Area formula
Area = width × length = _____ × (x + 3) = _______(x + 3)
③ EXPAND
4(x + 3) = 4 × _____ + 4 × _____ = _____________
④ SOLVE — Substitute x = 5
4(___) + 12 = _____ + 12 = _____ cm²
⑤ ANSWER
The area of the rectangle is _____ cm² when x = 5.
✅ Full Solution
① Read: write area formula, expand, then evaluate at x = 5
② Define: width = 4, length = (x + 3), x = unknown length in cm
③ Write: Area = 4(x + 3)
③ Expand: 4(x + 3) = 4x + 12
④ Solve: when x = 5: 4(5) + 12 = 20 + 12 = 32 cm²
⑤ Answer: The area is 32 cm² when x = 5.

Note: You could also substitute directly into 4(x + 3): 4(5 + 3) = 4 × 8 = 32. Both forms give the same result — they are equivalent expressions.

Problem 2 — Sharing Stickers

2-step
Three friends each have a collection of stickers. Each friend has n stickers plus 5 bonus stickers from a prize pack. Write a bracketed expression for the total number of stickers across all three friends, then expand and simplify.
① READ
Each friend has _____ stickers total. There are _____ friends. I need the total for all of them.
② DEFINE
n = number of _____________ stickers each friend has (before the bonus)
③ WRITE — Each friend has (n + 5) stickers
Total = _____ × (n + 5) = _______(n + 5)
③ EXPAND
3(n + 5) = 3 × _____ + 3 × _____ = _____________
⑤ ANSWER
The total number of stickers is _____________ (in terms of n).
✅ Full Solution
① Read: each friend has n + 5 stickers; 3 friends total
② Define: n = base sticker count per friend
③ Write: Total = 3(n + 5)
③ Expand: 3 × n = 3n; 3 × 5 = 15 → 3n + 15
⑤ Answer: Total stickers = 3n + 15

The expanded form 3n + 15 shows clearly: 3n stickers from the base collection and 15 bonus stickers in total (3 friends × 5 each).

Problem 3 — Plumber's Quote

Multi-step
A plumber charges $50 as a call-out fee, plus $t per hour for work. A particular job takes 3 hours. The same plumber is booked for two identical jobs in one day. Write and expand an expression for the total charge for both jobs.
① READ — Two stages: one job, then two jobs
First find the cost of _____ job, then multiply by _____ for the total.
② DEFINE
t = the plumber's _____________ rate in dollars per hour
③ WRITE — Cost of one job
One job = call-out + hourly rate × hours = _____ + _____ × _____ = _____t + 50
③ WRITE — Total for two identical jobs
Two jobs = 2 × (one job) = 2(_____t + 50)
④ EXPAND
2(3t + 50) = 2 × _____ + 2 × _____ = _____________
⑤ ANSWER
The total charge for two jobs is $_____________ (in terms of t).
✅ Full Solution
① Read: find one-job cost first, then two jobs
② Define: t = hourly rate ($/hr)
③ One job: 3t + 50 (3 hours at rate t, plus $50 call-out)
③ Two jobs: 2(3t + 50)
④ Expand: 2 × 3t = 6t; 2 × 50 = 100 → 6t + 100
⑤ Answer: Total charge = $6t + $100

The expanded form shows: $6t for 6 total hours of work, and $100 for two call-out fees. If t = 80: 6(80) + 100 = $580 total.

✅ Self-Marking Practice

Foundation: Understand grouping symbols and expand single brackets with positive factors.
Q1. What does 2(x + 3) equal?
The bracket means multiply both terms inside by 2. Two separate multiplications: 2 × x, and 2 × 3.
Q2. Expand   3(a + 5)
Two multiplications: 3 × a = ? and 3 × 5 = ?
Q3. Expand   4(2x − 1)
4 × 2x: multiply the number 4 by the coefficient 2. Then 4 × (−1) for the second term.
Q4. Which expression is equivalent to 5(n − 2)?
Expand 5(n − 2): multiply 5 by n, then multiply 5 by −2. What is 5 × (−2)?
Core: Expand and simplify with collecting like terms; introduce negative factors.
Q1. Expand and simplify   3(x + 2) + 5x
Step 1: expand 3(x + 2) to get two terms. Step 2: write all terms out. Step 3: collect any like terms.
Q2. Expand   −4(a − 3)
Two multiplications: (−4) × a, and (−4) × (−3). What sign does negative × negative give?
Q3. Expand and simplify   2(m + 4) + 3(m − 1)
Expand each bracket separately first. Then collect all m-terms and all constants.
Q4. A rectangle has length (2a + 3) and width 5. Which expression gives the area after expanding?
Area = length × width = (2a + 3) × 5 = 5(2a + 3). Expand this using the distributive law.
Extension: Double expansion, negative coefficients, subtraction of brackets, and the invisible −1 factor.
Q1. Expand and simplify   3(2x − 4) − 2(x + 1)
The minus sign before 2(x + 1) distributes to both terms inside. Think of it as −2(x + 1): −2 × x = −2x and −2 × 1 = −2.
Q2. Which of these is equivalent to −2(3 − m)?
Two multiplications: (−2) × 3 and (−2) × (−m). Apply the sign rules to each carefully.
Q3. Expand and simplify   4(x + 2) − (x + 3)
The bracket −(x + 3) has an invisible factor of −1. Write it as −1(x + 3) and distribute: −1 × x = −x and −1 × 3 = −3.
Q4. A square has side length (n + 3) cm. Which expression gives the perimeter after expanding?
A square has 4 equal sides, each of length (n + 3). Perimeter = 4 × (n + 3). Expand this expression.
