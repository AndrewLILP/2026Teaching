Definition

A fraction represents equal parts of a whole. The numerator (top) shows how many parts we have. The denominator (bottom) shows how many equal parts the whole is divided into.

Examples

34,  12,  710,  58

Non-examples

Key Idea

The denominator must never be zero — you can't divide into zero equal parts!

Simplest Form
lowest terms / fully reduced

Definition

A fraction is in simplest form (or lowest terms) when the numerator and denominator share no common factors other than 1.

Examples

34, 57, 29 — HCF of top and bottom is 1

Non-examples

68 (÷2 gives 34), 1015 (÷5 gives 23)

Key Idea

Divide both numerator and denominator by their HCF to reach simplest form in one step.

📌 Highest Common Factor (HCF)

The HCF of two numbers is the largest number that divides evenly into both. We use it to simplify fractions in one step.

Step 1List all factors of each number
Step 2Identify factors that appear in both lists
Step 3Select the largest common factor
💡
Example: Find HCF(18, 24)
Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
Factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24
Common factors: 1, 2, 3, 6 → HCF = 6
⚠️
Misconception watch: Students often list multiples instead of factors. Remember — factors divide into the number; multiples are the number multiplied by others.
🔁 Generating Equivalent Fractions

An infinite number of equivalent fractions exist for any fraction. You generate them by multiplying or dividing both parts by the same number.

Method How it works Example
✖️ Multiply Multiply numerator AND denominator by the same number 23 × 44 = 812
➗ Divide Divide numerator AND denominator by a common factor 1015 ÷ 55 = 23
🧱
Visual model: A fraction wall or number line shows that 12 = 24 = 48 by shading equal lengths.
✂️ Simplifying Fractions

Simplifying (or reducing) a fraction means writing it in its lowest terms — smallest possible numerator and denominator.

Method A — One StepFind HCF of numerator & denominator, then divide both by it
Method B — RepeatedDivide by any common factor repeatedly until no factors remain
📐
Method A: Simplify 1824
HCF(18, 24) = 6 → 18 ÷ 624 ÷ 6 = 34 ✅

Method B: Simplify 1824
÷ 2 → 912 → ÷ 3 → 34 ✅
✅
How to check: The HCF of your final numerator and denominator should be 1. If it isn't, keep simplifying!
⚖️ Comparing & Ordering Fractions

To reliably compare fractions with different denominators, convert them to a common denominator first — then compare numerators.

Step 1Find the LCM of the denominators (the LCD)
Step 2Convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with the LCD
Step 3Compare the numerators — larger numerator = larger fraction
📊
Example: Which is larger, 34 or 57?
LCD of 4 and 7 = 28
34 = 2128  |  57 = 2028
Since 21 > 20, we have 34 > 57
⚠️
Common mistake: Students compare only numerators or only denominators. A larger denominator means smaller pieces — you must convert to a common denominator first.

📋 Key Rules — Equivalence & Simplification

  • Multiply or divide both numerator and denominator by the same non-zero number to create an equivalent fraction.
  • Dividing by the HCF simplifies a fraction to lowest terms in a single step.
  • You can check your simplification: if HCF(numerator, denominator) = 1, you're done.
  • On the fraction wall, equivalent fractions line up at exactly the same point — they cover the same length of the whole.
  • A fraction with a larger denominator has smaller individual pieces — more pieces needed to cover the same amount.

🔍 Case 1 — Simplifying a Fraction
A student simplified 3045. Find the line containing the error.
Line 1 Factors of 30: 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 10, 15, 30
Line 2 Factors of 45: 1, 3, 5, 9, 15, 45
Line 3 Common factors: 1, 3, 5, 15 → HCF = 5
Line 4 30 ÷ 545 ÷ 5 = 69
Line 5 HCF(6, 9) = 3, so this is not simplest form yet. Final answer: 23
🎯 Error Type: Incorrect HCF Identification

Line 3 is wrong. The common factors of 30 and 45 are 1, 3, 5, and 15 — and 15 is the largest, so HCF(30, 45) = 15, not 5.

Because the wrong HCF was used (5 instead of 15), the fraction only simplified to 69 rather than reaching simplest form directly. The student correctly noticed further simplification was needed in Line 5 — but using HCF = 15 would give the answer in one step.

✅ Correct working: HCF(30, 45) = 15 → 30 ÷ 1545 ÷ 15 = 23
🔍 Case 2 — Comparing Fractions
A student compared 23 and 35 to decide which is larger. Find the error.
Line 1 I need a common denominator for 3 and 5. LCM(3, 5) = 15.
Line 2 23 = 1015 (multiplied numerator and denominator by 5)
Line 3 35 = 815 (multiplied numerator and denominator by 3)
Line 4 Comparing: 1015 vs 815 → 10 > 8
Line 5 Therefore 23 > 35
🎯 Error Type: Incorrect Equivalent Fraction — Multiplication Error

Line 3 is wrong. To convert 35 to fifteenths, you multiply both parts by 3: 3 × 3 = 9 (not 8) and 5 × 3 = 15. The denominator was converted correctly but the numerator was computed incorrectly (possibly adding instead of multiplying).

✅ Correct: 35 = 915 → Compare 1015 vs 915 → 23 > 35 ✅ (conclusion unchanged, but for the right reason)
📖 Problem 1 — Foundation
A pizza is cut into 12 equal slices. Mia eats 4 slices and Raj eats 3 slices.
Write each person's share as a fraction in simplest form. Who ate more?
1
READ — What is the problem about?
This problem is about _______________ cut into _______________ equal pieces.
2
DEFINE — What do I need to find?
I need to find each person's share as a fraction, then _______________ them.
3
WRITE — Set up the mathematics
Mia's share = ___/12    Raj's share = ___/12    To simplify: find HCF(numerator, 12)
4
SOLVE — Work it out
HCF(4,12) = ___ → Mia = ___/___   HCF(3,12) = ___ → Raj = ___/___
5
ANSWER — Write a sentence
Mia ate _______________ and Raj ate _______________ of the pizza. _______________ ate more because _______________.
Mia's share: 412 → HCF(4, 12) = 4 → 4÷412÷4 = 13

Raj's share: 312 → HCF(3, 12) = 3 → 3÷312÷3 = 14

Comparing: Common denominator of 3 and 4 is 12 → 13 = 412 and 14 = 312 → since 4 > 3, Mia ate more.

Answer: Mia ate 13 of the pizza and Raj ate 14. Mia ate more because 13 > 14.
📖 Problem 2 — Core
Three students each ran a section of a relay race. Aisha ran 38 of the total distance, Ben ran 512, and Chloe ran the rest. Arrange Aisha and Ben's fractions in order from smallest to largest, then determine what fraction Chloe ran.
1
READ — What is this problem about?
This problem is about _______________ sharing a total distance of _______________ between three runners.
2
DEFINE — What do I need to find?
I need to (1) order _______________ and _______________ from smallest to largest, and (2) find Chloe's fraction by _______________.
3
WRITE — Set up the mathematics
LCD(8, 12) = ___   Aisha: 38 = ?24   Ben: 512 = ?24
4
SOLVE — Work it out
Aisha = ___/24, Ben = ___/24 → order: ___ < ___   Chloe = 1 − 38 − 512 = ___
5
ANSWER — Write a sentence
From smallest to largest: _______________. Chloe ran _______________ of the total distance.
LCD(8, 12) = 24
Aisha: 38 = 924   Ben: 512 = 1024

Order: 924 < 1024 → Aisha < Ben

Chloe's fraction: 1 − 924 − 1024 = 2424 − 924 − 1024 = 524

Answer: From smallest to largest: Aisha (38) then Ben (512). Chloe ran 524 of the total distance.
📖 Problem 3 — Extension
A school garden is divided into sections. The vegetable patch covers 25 of the garden, the herb garden covers 38, and the rest is lawn. A student claims that the herb garden is larger than the vegetable patch. Is this claim correct? Show your reasoning, and express the lawn as a fraction in simplest form.
1
READ — What is this problem about?
This problem involves three sections of a garden: _______________, _______________, and _______________.
2
DEFINE — What do I need to find?
I need to (1) verify whether _______________ > _______________ and (2) find the fraction for _______________.
3
WRITE — Set up the mathematics
LCD(5, 8) = ___   25 = ?40   38 = ?40   Lawn = 1 − ___ − ___
4
SOLVE — Work it out
Vegetable = ___/40   Herb = ___/40   Compare: ___ ? ___   Lawn = ___/40 = ___ (simplest form)
5
ANSWER — Write a sentence
The student's claim is _______________ because _______________. The lawn covers _______________ of the garden.
LCD(5, 8) = 40
Vegetable: 25 = 1640   Herb: 38 = 1540

Comparison: 1640 > 1540 → The vegetable patch is actually larger.

Lawn: 1 − 1640 − 1540 = 940 (HCF(9,40) = 1 → already simplest form)

Answer: The student's claim is incorrect. The herb garden (38 = 1540) is smaller than the vegetable patch (25 = 1640). The lawn covers 940 of the garden.
