📚 Vocabulary — tap each card to flip
Rate
TAP TO FLIP ↩
A comparison of two quantities measured in different units.Written with a slash or the word "per": km/h, $/kg, mL per min.
Examples
60 km/h, $4.50/kg, 15 mL per min, 220 beats/min
Non-examples
3:5 (ratio — same units), $4.50 (single quantity)
Key idea
A rate always has TWO different units
Unit rate
TAP TO FLIP ↩
A rate where the second quantity is exactly 1.Found by dividing the first quantity by the second quantity.
Examples
75 km per 1 hour, $2.40 per 1 kg, 5 mL per 1 min
Non-examples
300 km in 4 h (not simplified), $7.20 for 3 kg (not simplified)
Key idea
Divide the first number by the second to get unit rate
Per
TAP TO FLIP ↩
The word meaning "for each one" that signals a rate. It means ÷ (divide).The slash symbol / means the same thing as "per".
Examples
"60 km per hour" = 60 km/h. "$3 per litre" = $3/L.
Non-examples
"60 km and 1 hour" — the word "and" doesn't make a rate
Key idea
"Per" = ÷ (the first unit divided by the second)
Speed
TAP TO FLIP ↩
A rate that compares distance and time.Speed = distance ÷ time. Common units: km/h and m/s.
Examples
100 km/h (car), 10 m/s (sprint), 340 m/s (sound)
Non-examples
100 km (distance only — no time), 2 hours (time only)
Key idea
km/h ÷ 3.6 = m/s  |  m/s × 3.6 = km/h
Best buy
TAP TO FLIP ↩
The product that gives the lowest price per unit quantity.Compare by converting all options to the same unit first.
Examples
500 g for $4 (80¢/100g) vs 2 kg for $14 (70¢/100g) → 2 kg wins
Non-examples
Comparing total prices without converting to same unit
Key idea
Always find price per common unit before comparing
Conversion
TAP TO FLIP ↩
Changing a rate from one set of units to another equivalent set of units.Multiply or divide by conversion factors (1 km = 1000 m; 1 h = 3600 s).
Examples
90 km/h → 25 m/s (÷ 3.6). 5 mL/min → 0.3 L/hr (× 60 ÷ 1000)
Non-examples
Changing only one part of the rate (e.g. km → m but keeping hours)
Key idea
Change BOTH units simultaneously — top and bottom
🔗 Connection to Chapter 1: In Chapter 1 you compared quantities in the same units using ratios. Rates extend that idea — they compare quantities in different units. Everything you know about simplifying and the unitary method still applies.
💡 Rate vs Ratio — what's different?

Ratio (3 : 5)

  • Compares quantities in the same units
  • Units cancel out — no units in the answer
  • Written with a colon
  • Example: 12 red tiles to 18 blue tiles = 2:3

Rate (60 km/h)

  • Compares quantities in different units
  • Units remain — always written in the answer
  • Written with a slash or the word "per"
  • Example: 300 km in 4 hours = 75 km/h
💡 Writing and simplifying a rate

Two steps to express any rate

Step 1 — Write the rate with both units included. Always name both quantities and their units.

Step 2 — Simplify to a unit rate by dividing both quantities so the second becomes 1.

Example: A tap fills 240 litres in 3 hours.
Rate = 240 L ÷ 3 h = 80 L/h (80 litres per hour, a unit rate)
⚠️ Always write units in your answer. "80" on its own is meaningless — "80 L/h" tells the full story.

💡 Financial rates — best buys

Three steps to find the best buy

  1. Convert all quantities to the same unit (e.g. grams, not kg and g mixed)
  2. Calculate the price per unit for each option (price ÷ quantity)
  3. Compare — the lowest price per unit is the best buy
Example: 500 g for $4.50 vs 2 kg for $14.00
Option A: $4.50 ÷ 500 = $0.009/g = $0.90 per 100 g
Option B: $14.00 ÷ 2000 = $0.007/g = $0.70 per 100 g
Option B is the best buy even though it costs more in total.
⚠️ Never compare total prices directly — a bigger pack almost always costs more in total but less per unit.

📌 Key rules for best buy comparisons

  1. Convert to the same unit first — you can't compare g and kg directly.
  2. Calculate price per unit — always price ÷ quantity (not quantity ÷ price).
  3. Lower price per unit = better value, even if the total price is higher.
  4. Consider practicality — a bigger pack may be better value but might expire before use. Maths tells you the rate; you decide the best choice.
  5. Watch for misleading promotions — "Buy 2 get 1 free" changes the unit rate calculation.
✏️ Worked Examples — reveal one step at a time
Foundation A car travels 300 km in 4 hours. Express this as a unit rate in km/h.
Step 1
Identify the two quantities and their units
Distance = 300 km  |  Time = 4 hours
A rate always has two different units — here, km and hours.
Step 2
Write as a rate fraction
300 km4 h
Write the first quantity over the second, keeping units on both.
Step 3
Divide to find the unit rate (denominator = 1)
300 ÷ 4 = 75 km/h
Dividing both quantities by 4 gives 75 km per 1 hour — a unit rate.
Step 4 — State clearly
Write the answer with units
The car's speed is 75 km/h
Always include units in the final answer. "75" alone doesn't answer the question.
Foundation → Core A tap drips 15 mL every 3 minutes. Express this as a rate in mL/min, then convert to L/hr.
Step 1
Find the unit rate in mL/min
15 mL ÷ 3 min = 5 mL/min
Divide both quantities by 3 so the time denominator becomes 1 minute.
Step 2
Convert minutes to hours: multiply by 60
5 mL/min × 60 = 300 mL per hour
There are 60 minutes in 1 hour. Multiplying by 60 scales the rate up to "per hour".
Step 3
Convert mL to L: divide by 1000
300 mL ÷ 1000 = 0.3 L per hour
There are 1000 mL in 1 litre. Dividing by 1000 converts the volume unit.
Step 4 — State both rates
Write the two equivalent rates
5 mL/min  =  0.3 L/hr
Both describe the same drip rate — one in smaller units, one in larger units.
Core Convert 90 km/h to m/s.
Step 1
Convert the distance unit: km → m
90 km = 90 × 1000 m = 90 000 m per hour
1 km = 1000 m, so multiply the 90 by 1000 to convert kilometres to metres.
Step 2
Convert the time unit: hours → seconds
1 hour = 60 × 60 = 3600 seconds
1 hour = 60 minutes, and 1 minute = 60 seconds, so 1 hour = 3600 seconds.
Step 3
Divide to get metres per second
90 000 m ÷ 3600 s = 25 m/s
Divide the distance in metres by the time in seconds to get the speed in m/s.
Step 4 — Shortcut check
Verify using the ÷ 3.6 shortcut
90 ÷ 3.6 = 25 m/s ✓
The shortcut works because 1000 ÷ 3600 = 1 ÷ 3.6. Always divide km/h by 3.6 to get m/s.
Core → Extension Which is better value: 750 g of coffee for $4.50, or 1.2 kg for $6.84?
Step 1
Convert all quantities to the same unit (grams)
Option A: 750 g  |  Option B: 1.2 kg = 1.2 × 1000 = 1200 g
You can't compare prices directly unless the quantities use the same unit.
Step 2
Calculate the unit price for Option A (per 100 g)
$4.50 ÷ 750 g = $0.006/g  →  $0.006 × 100 = $0.60 per 100 g
Dividing price by quantity gives price per gram. Multiplying by 100 makes the comparison easier to read.
Step 3
Calculate the unit price for Option B (per 100 g)
$6.84 ÷ 1200 g = $0.0057/g  →  $0.0057 × 100 = $0.57 per 100 g
Same process for Option B — price divided by quantity in grams, then scaled to per 100 g.
Step 4 — Compare and conclude
Compare unit prices and state the best buy
Option A: $0.60/100 g  |  Option B: $0.57/100 g  →  Option B is better value
$0.57 < $0.60, so Option B (1.2 kg) costs less per 100 g — it is the better buy.
Extension A worker earns $18.40/hr on weekdays and time-and-a-half on weekends. Calculate their total pay for 8 hours on Monday and 5 hours on Saturday.
Step 1
Find the weekend (Saturday) pay rate
Time-and-a-half = $18.40 × 1.5 = $27.60/hr
"Time-and-a-half" means 1.5 times the base rate. Multiply $18.40 by 1.5.
Step 2
Calculate Monday (weekday) earnings
8 hrs × $18.40/hr = $147.20
Multiply hours worked by the weekday rate.
Step 3
Calculate Saturday (weekend) earnings
5 hrs × $27.60/hr = $138.00
Multiply hours worked by the weekend (time-and-a-half) rate.
Step 4 — Total pay
Add both days' earnings
$147.20 + $138.00 = $285.20
Total pay = weekday pay + weekend pay. Note that 5 Saturday hours earns almost as much as 8 Monday hours because of the higher rate.
🔍 Error Detective — find the mistake

Each worked solution below contains exactly one error. Click the line you think is wrong, then reveal the answer to check.

Case 1 Convert 108 km/h to m/s
L1 Convert km to m: 108 × 1000 = 108 000 m per hour
L2 1 hour = 3600 seconds
L3 Speed in m/s = 108 000 × 3600 = 388 800 000 m/s
L4 Answer: 388 800 000 m/s
Error on Line 3 — Common misconception: multiplying by 3600 instead of dividing

What went wrong: To convert from "per hour" to "per second", you divide by 3600 (because there are 3600 seconds in 1 hour — you're splitting the hour into 3600 smaller pieces). Multiplying by 3600 is the opposite and gives an impossibly large answer.

Spot check: 388 800 000 m/s is faster than the speed of light — a clear sign something went wrong!

✅ Correct: 108 000 m ÷ 3600 s = 30 m/s
Shortcut check: 108 km/h ÷ 3.6 = 30 m/s ✓
Case 2 Which is better value: 400 g for $3.60, or 2 kg for $16.00?
L1 Option A: 400 g for $3.60. Option B: 2 kg for $16.00.
L2 Comparing prices: $3.60 < $16.00, so Option A is cheaper.
L3 Therefore, Option A is better value.
L4 Answer: Buy Option A (400 g).
Error on Line 2 — Common misconception: comparing total price instead of unit price

What went wrong: Comparing total prices ($3.60 vs $16.00) ignores that the two packs contain very different amounts. To find best value, you must calculate the price for the same quantity and compare those.

✅ Correct working:
Option A: $3.60 ÷ 400 g = $0.009/g = $0.90 per 100 g
Option B: $16.00 ÷ 2000 g = $0.008/g = $0.80 per 100 g
Option B is better value despite costing more in total.
📖 Word Problems — Newman's five steps

Work through each step using the sentence frames as a guide. When you're ready, reveal the full solution.

Foundation — 1 step
A cyclist travels 48 km in 90 minutes. What is their speed in km/h?
1Read
I need to find: the speed in km/h
2Define the key information
Distance = 48 km  |  Time = 90 minutes  |  Target unit = km/h
3Write the equation
First convert time to hours: 90 min = ___ ÷ 60 = ___ hours
Speed = distance ÷ time = ___ ÷ ___
4Solve
90 ÷ 60 = ___ hours  |  48 ÷ ___ = ___ km/h
5Answer in a sentence
The cyclist's speed is ___ km/h.

Step 3: Convert time: 90 min ÷ 60 = 1.5 hours

Step 4: Speed = 48 ÷ 1.5 = 32 km/h

Step 5: The cyclist's speed is 32 km/h.

Core — 2 steps
Petrol is priced at 198.4 cents per litre. Estimate the cost to fill a 52-litre tank, then give the exact cost in dollars.
1Read
I need to find: the estimated cost, and the exact cost in dollars, for 52 L of petrol
2Define the key information
Rate = 198.4 c/L  |  Volume = 52 L  |  Convert answer to dollars ($)
3Estimate first
Round to nearest 10s: rate ≈ 200 c/L, volume ≈ 50 L
Estimated cost ≈ 200 × 50 = ___ cents = $___
4Solve exactly
Exact cost = 198.4 × 52 = ___ cents
Convert to dollars: ___ ÷ 100 = $___
5Answer in a sentence
It will cost approximately $___ to fill the tank. The exact cost is $___.

Step 3 (Estimate): 200 c/L × 50 L = 10 000 c ≈ $100

Step 4 (Exact): 198.4 × 52 = 10 316.8 cents ÷ 100 = $103.17 (rounded to nearest cent)

Step 5: It will cost approximately $100 to fill the tank. The exact cost is $103.17.

Note: The estimate of $100 is close to the exact answer of $103.17 — this confirms we haven't made a major error.

Extension — multi-step
Two supermarkets sell the same brand of olive oil. Supermarket X sells 750 mL for $8.40. Supermarket Y sells 2 L for $21.50, but members get a 15% discount. Which supermarket offers better value for members of Supermarket Y?
1Read
I need to find: the unit price at each supermarket (after Y's discount) and compare them
2Define the key information
X: 750 mL for $8.40  |  Y: 2 L for $21.50, less 15%
3Write the equations
Y's discounted price = $21.50 × (1 − 0.15) = $21.50 × ___ = $___
Convert Y's volume to mL: 2 L = ___ mL
4Solve — find unit price per 100 mL
X: $8.40 ÷ 750 × 100 = $___ per 100 mL
Y: $___ ÷ 2000 × 100 = $___ per 100 mL
5Answer in a sentence
For members of Supermarket Y, Supermarket ___ offers better value at $___ per 100 mL compared to $___ per 100 mL at Supermarket ___.

Step 3: Y's discounted price = $21.50 × 0.85 = $18.275 (round to $18.28)

Y's volume: 2 L = 2000 mL

Step 4:

Supermarket X: $8.40 ÷ 750 × 100 = $1.12 per 100 mL

Supermarket Y (discounted): $18.275 ÷ 2000 × 100 = $0.914 per 100 mL (≈ $0.91)

Step 5: For Supermarket Y members, Supermarket Y offers better value at approximately $0.91 per 100 mL compared to $1.12 per 100 mL at Supermarket X.

Q1. Which of the following is a rate?
A rate always involves two different units of measurement joined by the word "per" or a slash (/). Which option has two different units?
Q2. A garden hose fills 18 litres in 3 minutes. What is the flow rate as a unit rate?
Unit rate means the time denominator = 1. Divide both quantities by the time (3 minutes): 18 ÷ 3 = ?
Q3. A printer prints 240 pages in 8 minutes. Which correctly expresses this as a unit rate?
Unit rate: divide pages by minutes so the answer is "pages per 1 minute". 240 ÷ 8 = ?
Q4. What is the correct unit for a rate comparing mass (grams) and cost (dollars)?
The rate unit is written as: (first unit) per (second unit). "Per" means ÷. If the rate is cost ÷ mass, the unit is dollars ÷ grams = $/g.
Q1. Convert 72 km/h to m/s.
To convert km/h to m/s: divide by 3.6. Or in two steps: multiply by 1000 (km→m), then divide by 3600 (h→s).
Q2. A plumber charges $85 per hour. How much will a job taking 2.5 hours cost?
Cost = rate × time. Multiply $85 by 2.5. Remember: 0.5 of an hour = 30 minutes.
Q3. Two bags of rice: 2 kg for $5.60, or 5 kg for $13.50. Which is better value?
Calculate the price per kg for each: $5.60 ÷ 2 and $13.50 ÷ 5. Compare — which is lower?
Q4. A cyclist rides at 15 m/s. How long does it take (in minutes) to travel 9 km?
Speed is in m/s, so convert 9 km to metres first. Then: time = distance ÷ speed (in seconds). Finally, convert seconds to minutes.
Q1. Convert 25 m/s to km/h.
m/s → km/h: multiply by 3.6. The number always gets bigger (km/h numbers are larger than m/s for the same speed).
Q2. Three bottles of shampoo: 300 mL for $3.90, 750 mL for $9.00, 1.5 L for $16.50. Which is best value?
Convert 1.5 L to mL first. Then find $/100 mL for each bottle: price ÷ quantity (in mL) × 100. Compare all three.
Q3. A casual worker earns $22.50/hr normally and double time on public holidays. She works 6 hours at normal rate and 3 hours on a public holiday. What is her total pay?
Double time = 2 × base rate. Calculate normal pay and public holiday pay separately, then add them.
Q4. A water leak drips at 8 mL per second. How many litres will have leaked after 1 hour?
1 hour = 3600 seconds. Multiply: 8 mL × 3600 = total mL. Then divide by 1000 to convert to litres.
