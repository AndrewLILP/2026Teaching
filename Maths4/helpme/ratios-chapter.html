💡 What is a ratio?

Comparing quantities in the same units

A ratio compares two or more quantities that are measured in the same units. We write ratios using a colon ( : ) and read them as "to".

Example: A bag has 4 red marbles and 6 blue marbles. The ratio of red to blue is 4:6, read as "4 to 6".

⚠️ Order matters! Red to Blue (4:6) is different from Blue to Red (6:4). Always match the order in the question.
💡 Part vs Whole

Part-to-Part

Compares one share to another share.

Example: In 3:5, the first group has 3 parts and the second group has 5 parts.

The 3 and 5 are both parts.

Part-to-Whole

Expresses one share as a fraction of the total.

Example: In 3:5, the whole = 3 + 5 = 8. The first part is 38 of the whole.

Key idea: Each part of a ratio is a fraction of the whole. In a ratio of a : b, the first part is aa+b and the second part is ba+b of the whole.
💡 Simplifying a ratio

Step-by-step process

To simplify a ratio, divide all parts by their highest common factor (HCF).

💡 Ratio vs Fraction — what's different?

Ratio (3:5)

  • Compares two groups to each other
  • Can have 3 or more parts (e.g. 2:3:7)
  • Written with a colon
  • 3:5 means "3 parts to 5 parts"

Fraction (38)

  • Compares one part to the whole
  • Always two numbers (numerator, denominator)
  • Written with a fraction bar
  • 38 means "3 parts out of 8 total"
💡 Dividing a quantity in a given ratio — Unitary method

Three steps

  1. Find the total number of parts — add all the numbers in the ratio
  2. Find the value of one part — divide the total quantity by the total parts
  3. Find each share — multiply the value of one part by each ratio number
Example: Divide $240 in the ratio 3:5.
Total parts = 3 + 5 = 8  |  1 part = $240 ÷ 8 = $30
First share = 3 × $30 = $90  |  Second share = 5 × $30 = $150
✔ Check: $90 + $150 = $240 ✓

📌 Key rules to remember

  1. All parts of a ratio must use the same units.
  2. To find the value of each part: total ÷ total parts gives "1 part".
  3. Each part as a fraction of the whole: part ÷ (sum of all parts).
  4. The sum of all fractional parts always equals 1 (the whole).
  5. Equivalent ratios have the same bar shape — just scaled differently.
✏️ Worked Examples — reveal one step at a time
Foundation Simplify the ratio 12:18.
Step 1
Find the HCF of 12 and 18
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12  |  Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
The HCF is the largest number that divides evenly into both parts.
Step 2
Divide both parts by 6
12 ÷ 6 : 18 ÷ 6 = 2 : 3
Dividing all parts by the HCF gives the simplest form.
Step 3 — Check
Can 2:3 be simplified further?
HCF(2, 3) = 1  →  No further simplification possible.
When the only common factor is 1, the ratio is in its simplest form. ✓
Foundation → Core In a class the ratio of boys to girls is 3:7. What fraction of the class are boys?
Step 1
Find the total number of parts
3 + 7 = 10 parts
The whole is the sum of all parts in the ratio.
Step 2
Write boys' part as a fraction of the whole
Boys = 310 of the class
The numerator is the boys' part (3); the denominator is the total parts (10).
Step 3 — Check
Verify: girls' fraction + boys' fraction = 1
310 + 710 = 1010 = 1 ✓
The fractions of all parts must add to 1 (the whole class).
Core Divide $240 in the ratio 3:5.
Step 1
Find the total number of parts
3 + 5 = 8 parts
Add all numbers in the ratio to find the whole.
Step 2
Find the value of one part (unitary method)
$240 ÷ 8 = $30 per part
Dividing the total by the number of parts gives us the unit value.
Step 3
Calculate each share
First share: 3 × $30 = $90  |  Second share: 5 × $30 = $150
Multiply the unit value by each part number in the ratio.
Step 4 — Check
Verify the shares add to the total
$90 + $150 = $240 ✓
Always check that your shares add back to the original quantity.
Core → Extension A recipe uses flour and sugar in the ratio 5:2. If you use 350 g of flour, how much sugar is needed?
Step 1
Identify which part is known
Flour : Sugar = 5 : 2  |  Flour = 350 g (this is the "5 parts" quantity)
Read the ratio carefully — flour is the first quantity (5 parts).
Step 2
Find the value of one part
5 parts = 350 g  →  1 part = 350 ÷ 5 = 70 g
The unitary method: find what 1 part equals by dividing the known quantity by its ratio number.
Step 3
Calculate the sugar quantity
Sugar = 2 parts = 2 × 70 g = 140 g
Multiply 1 part by the ratio number for sugar (which is 2).
Step 4 — Check
Verify: flour ÷ sugar should equal 5 ÷ 2
350 ÷ 140 = 2.5  |  5 ÷ 2 = 2.5 ✓
The ratio of actual quantities should equal the original ratio.
Extension Three people invest in a business in the ratio 2:3:7. The total profit is $4 800. How much does each person receive?
Step 1
Find the total number of parts
2 + 3 + 7 = 12 parts
With three parts in the ratio, add all three numbers.
Step 2
Find the value of one part
$4 800 ÷ 12 = $400 per part
Dividing the total by 12 gives the unit value for a single part.
Step 3
Calculate each person's share
Person A (2 parts): 2 × $400 = $800
Person B (3 parts): 3 × $400 = $1 200
Person C (7 parts): 7 × $400 = $2 800
Multiply the unit value by each person's ratio number.
Step 4 — Check
Verify all shares sum to the total profit
$800 + $1 200 + $2 800 = $4 800 ✓
The three shares must add back to the original total.

Case 1 Simplify the ratio 15:25
L1 Factors of 15: 1, 3, 5, 15  |  Factors of 25: 1, 5, 25
L2 HCF(15, 25) = 5
L3 15 ÷ 5 : 25  =  3 : 25    ← only one part was divided
L4 Simplest form: 3:25
Error found on Line 3 — Common misconception: only dividing one part

What went wrong: The student divided 15 by the HCF (5) but forgot to also divide 25. To simplify, you must divide every part by the same number.

✅ Correct working:  15 ÷ 5 : 25 ÷ 5 = 3 : 5   (simplest form)
Case 2 Divide $120 in the ratio 2:3
L1 Ratio is 2:3, total parts = 2 + 3 = 5
L2 First share = 2/3 × $120 = $80    ← wrong fraction used
L3 Second share = 3/3 × $120 = $120
L4 Check: $80 + $120 = $200 ✗ (not $120)
Error on Line 2 — Common misconception: using part:part fraction instead of part:whole

What went wrong: The student wrote the fraction as 2/3 (part A over part B). The correct fraction is each part over the total number of parts. The denominator should be 5 (= 2 + 3), not 3.

✅ Correct: First share = 25 × $120 = $48  |  Second share = 35 × $120 = $72  |  Check: $48 + $72 = $120 ✓
📖 Word Problems — Newman's five steps

Work through each step using the sentence frames as a guide. When you're ready, reveal the full solution.

Foundation — 1 step
A sports bag contains 12 blue socks and 18 white socks. Write and simplify the ratio of blue socks to white socks.
1Read
I need to find: the simplified ratio of blue to white socks
2Define the key information
Blue socks = ___  |  White socks = ___  |  Units are the same? Yes / No
3Write the ratio
Blue : White = ___ : ___
4Solve — simplify
HCF(___, ___) = ___  |  Simplified ratio = ___ : ___
5Answer in a sentence
The ratio of blue socks to white socks in simplest form is ___ : ___.

Step 1–3: Blue : White = 12 : 18

Step 4: HCF(12, 18) = 6  →  12 ÷ 6 : 18 ÷ 6 = 2 : 3

Step 5: The ratio of blue socks to white socks in simplest form is 2 : 3.

Core — 2 steps
A builder mixes cement, sand, and gravel in the ratio 1:2:4 to make concrete. How many kilograms of each material are needed to make 56 kg of concrete?
1Read
I need to find: the amount of cement, sand, and gravel in 56 kg of concrete
2Define the key information
Ratio = 1 : 2 : 4  |  Total quantity = 56 kg
3Write the equation
Total parts = 1 + 2 + 4 = ___  |  1 part = 56 ÷ ___ = ___ kg
4Solve
Cement (___ parts) = ___ × ___ = ___ kg
Sand (___ parts) = ___ × ___ = ___ kg
Gravel (___ parts) = ___ × ___ = ___ kg
5Answer in a sentence
To make 56 kg of concrete, you need ___ kg of cement, ___ kg of sand, and ___ kg of gravel.

Step 3: Total parts = 1 + 2 + 4 = 7  |  1 part = 56 ÷ 7 = 8 kg

Step 4:

Cement (1 part) = 1 × 8 = 8 kg

Sand (2 parts) = 2 × 8 = 16 kg

Gravel (4 parts) = 4 × 8 = 32 kg

✔ Check: 8 + 16 + 32 = 56 kg ✓

Step 5: To make 56 kg of concrete, you need 8 kg of cement, 16 kg of sand, and 32 kg of gravel.

Extension — multi-step
Mia and Noah share some prize money in the ratio 3:5. Mia receives $210. Later, a third friend Zara joins and the three of them re-share the same total in the ratio 3:5:4. How much does each person now receive, and how does Mia's new share compare to her original share?
1Read
I need to find: the total prize money, then each person's new share, then compare Mia's shares
2Define the key information
Original ratio: 3:5  |  Mia's original share: $210  |  New ratio: 3:5:4
3Write the equations
Original: Mia = 38 of total = $210  →  Total = $210 ÷ 38 = ___
4Solve
New total parts = 3+5+4 = ___  |  1 part = Total ÷ ___ = ___
Mia (3): ___  |  Noah (5): ___  |  Zara (4): ___
5Answer in a sentence
In the new arrangement, Mia receives ___, Noah receives ___, and Zara receives ___. Mia's new share is $___ more/less than her original share.

Step 3: Mia = 38 of total = $210  →  Total = $210 × 83 = $560

Step 4: New total parts = 3+5+4 = 12  |  1 part = $560 ÷ 12 = $46.67 (rounded to cents)

Mia (3 parts): 3 × $46.67 = $140

Noah (5 parts): 5 × $46.67 = $233.33

Zara (4 parts): 4 × $46.67 = $186.67

✔ Check: $140 + $233.33 + $186.67 = $560 ✓

Step 5: Mia now receives $140, Noah receives $233.33, and Zara receives $186.67. Mia's new share is $70 less than her original $210 — she received a smaller proportion when the prize was split three ways.

✅ Self-Marking Practice
Foundation: Identifying and writing ratios, simplifying two-part ratios, expressing parts as fractions of the whole.
Core: Applying the unitary method, dividing a quantity in a given ratio, solving one- and two-step ratio problems.
Extension: Three-part ratios, multi-step ratio problems, ratio problems embedded in complex real-life contexts.
Q1. A smoothie recipe uses 3 cups of strawberries and 5 cups of mango. What is the ratio of strawberries to mango?
The question asks "strawberries to mango" — write them in that order, separated by a colon.
Q2. Which is the simplest form of the ratio 20:30?
Find the HCF of 20 and 30, then divide both numbers by it. Check — can you simplify again?
Q3. In a class, the ratio of students who walk to school to those who take the bus is 4:6. What fraction of the class walk to school?
First find the whole (total parts). Then write walkers ÷ whole as a fraction. Can you simplify it?
Q4. Which ratio is equivalent to 3:4?
Multiply BOTH parts of 3:4 by the same whole number. Check each option by simplifying it — does it reduce to 3:4?
Q1. Divide $180 in the ratio 2:7. What is the larger share?
Step 1: Add the ratio numbers to get total parts. Step 2: $180 ÷ total parts = 1 part. Step 3: multiply by 7.
Q2. A cordial drink is made using water and cordial in the ratio 7:3. How many mL of cordial are needed to make 500 mL of drink?
Cordial is the second number in the ratio (3 parts). Find 1 part first: 500 ÷ (7+3). Then multiply by 3.
Q3. Jeans and a shirt are priced in the ratio 5:2. If the shirt costs $36, what is the price of the jeans?
The shirt = 2 parts. Divide $36 by 2 to find 1 part. Then multiply by 5 for the jeans.
Q4. A rectangle's length and width are in the ratio 5:2 and the perimeter is 56 cm. What is the length?
Perimeter = 2×(length+width). So length+width = 56÷2. Now divide that sum in the ratio 5:2 to find the length.
Q1. Three friends A, B, and C share a task in the ratio 2:3:5. If C completes 40 hours of work, how many hours did A and B complete in total?
C has 5 parts = 40 hours. Find 1 part, then find A (2 parts) and B (3 parts), and add them.
Q2. The ratio of cats to dogs at a shelter is 5:3. If 10 more cats arrive, the ratio becomes 5:2. How many dogs are at the shelter?
Let the number of groups be k. Write cats = 5k, dogs = 3k. After 10 more cats, the new ratio is (5k+10):3k = 5:2. Set up a cross-multiplication and solve.
Q3. Alloy X is made of copper and zinc in the ratio 3:2. Alloy Y is made of copper and zinc in the ratio 5:4. Which alloy has a higher proportion of copper?
Find the fraction of copper in each alloy: copper ÷ (copper + zinc). Convert to decimals to compare.
Q4. Two quantities P and Q are in the ratio 7:4. If P increases by 14 and Q stays the same, the new ratio is 3:1. What is the original value of Q?
Let P = 7k and Q = 4k. Write the new ratio as (7k + 14) : 4k = 3 : 1. Set up a cross-multiplication and solve for k, then find Q = 4k.
