Commutative Property

DefinitionChanging the order of terms or factors does not change the result. Applies to addition and multiplication.
Examples3x + 2y = 2y + 3x;   4 × a = a × 4;   5a × 3b = 3b × 5a
Non-examplesSubtraction: 5 − 3 ≠ 3 − 5; Division: 6 ÷ 2 ≠ 2 ÷ 6
Key ideaCommute = travel back and forth. The terms swap places but the result stays the same.
Associative Property
Grouping doesn't change the result

Associative Property

DefinitionChanging how terms are grouped (using brackets) does not change the result. Applies to addition and multiplication.
Examples(2a + 3a) + 5a = 2a + (3a + 5a);   (2 × 3) × a = 2 × (3 × a)
Non-examplesSubtraction: (8 − 3) − 2 ≠ 8 − (3 − 2); Division is also not associative.
Key ideaAssociate = group together. Regrouping the calculation does not change the answer.

Like Terms

DefinitionTerms that have exactly the same pronumeral part — same letters AND same powers. Only the coefficients may differ.
Examples3x and 7x;   −2ab and 5ab;   4x² and x²;   6 and −2 (both constants)
Non-examples3x and 3y (different pronumeral); 4x and 4x² (different power)
Key ideaThink of it like fruit: 3 apples + 2 apples = 5 apples. But you cannot add apples and oranges.

Unlike Terms

DefinitionTerms that have different pronumeral parts — different letters, or the same letter with different powers. Cannot be combined by adding or subtracting.
Examples3x and 2y;   5a and 4a²;   6x and 6 (constant)
Non-examples4n and 7n are NOT unlike — they share the same pronumeral part n¹.
Key ideaUnlike terms must be left as separate terms in a simplified expression.

Simplify / Collect

DefinitionTo rewrite an expression by combining all like terms, so it contains the fewest possible terms. The result is equivalent — same value for every substitution.
Examples4x + 3y + 2x = 6x + 3y;   5a − 2a + 1 = 3a + 1
Non-examples4x + 3y cannot be simplified further — the terms are unlike.
Key ideaAdd or subtract the coefficients. The pronumeral part does not change.

A — The Two Arithmetic Properties

↔️ Commutative Property

The order of terms can be changed without affecting the result. Works for addition and multiplication.

3x + 2y = 2y + 3x ✓ same result
5a × 3b = 3b × 5a ✓ same result
5a − 3b ≠ 3b − 5a ✗ subtraction is not commutative

( ) Associative Property

How terms are grouped can be changed without affecting the result. Works for addition and multiplication.

(2a + 3a) + 5a = 2a + (3a + 5a) ✓ both = 10a
(2 × 3) × a = 2 × (3 × a) ✓ both = 6a
(8 − 3) − 2 ≠ 8 − (3 − 2) ✗ subtraction is not associative
💡 Why do these matter in algebra? These properties are why we can rearrange and regroup terms when collecting like terms — we are applying the commutative and associative properties every time we do so.

B — Like Terms vs Unlike Terms

Like terms share the exact same pronumeral part — same letters, same powers. Colour-matching shows which can be combined:

3x + 2y + 5x − y + 4 → 8x + y + 4

The teal chips (x-terms) combine → 3x + 5x = 8x. The green chips (y-terms) combine → 2y − y = y. The amber chip (constant) has no partner and stays as is.

These terms look similar but are unlike — they cannot be combined:

4x and 4y (different pronumeral)
3x and 3x² (same letter, different power)
5x and 5 (one has a pronumeral, one is a constant)

Multiply the coefficients together, then list the pronumerals (alphabetically by convention).

3x × 4y = 12xy
5a × 2a = 10a²
2m × 3n × 4 = 24mn

Note: a × a = a² — repeated pronumeral becomes a power.

📌 Rules for collecting like terms

  • Identify like terms first — same pronumeral AND same power. The coefficient is irrelevant for identification.
  • Add or subtract the coefficients only — the pronumeral part does not change. 3x + 5x = 8x, not 8x².
  • Unlike terms are left separate — they form distinct terms in the simplified expression.
  • The commutative property lets you reorder terms before collecting. Always legal for addition.
  • A single term (e.g. 4n with no like partners) is already simplified — write it as is.

✏️ Worked Examples — reveal one step at a time

Example 1 — Commutative & Associative Properties

Foundation
Show that: (a)  3x + 5y = 5y + 3x    (b)  (2a + 3a) + 4a = 2a + (3a + 4a)
Part (a) — Apply the commutative property
3x + 5y = 5y + 3x
The commutative property of addition states that swapping the order of terms does not change the result. This is valid because addition is commutative — both arrangements represent the same total.
Part (b) — Evaluate the left side: group (2a + 3a) first
(2a + 3a) + 4a = 5a + 4a = 9a
Calculate inside the brackets first: 2a + 3a = 5a. Then add 4a to get 9a.
Part (b) — Evaluate the right side: group (3a + 4a) first
2a + (3a + 4a) = 2a + 7a = 9a
Calculate inside the brackets first: 3a + 4a = 7a. Then add 2a to get 9a. Both groupings give 9a — this confirms the associative property.
✅ The commutative and associative properties hold for algebraic addition — both are generalisations of the same number properties you know from arithmetic.

Example 2 — Collecting Like Terms

Foundation
Simplify  4x + 3y + 2x + y
Step 1 — Identify the like terms
4x and 2x are like terms  |  3y and y are like terms
Both 4x and 2x have the pronumeral part x. Both 3y and y have the pronumeral part y (the coefficient of y is 1 — invisible by convention).
Step 2 — Regroup using the commutative property
4x + 2x + 3y + y
Swap the order so like terms are adjacent. This uses the commutative property — the rearrangement does not change the value of the expression.
Step 3 — Add the coefficients of each like term group
(4 + 2)x + (3 + 1)y = 6x + 4y
Add only the coefficients. The pronumeral part (x or y) stays unchanged. 4x + 2x = 6x and 3y + 1y = 4y.
✅ Simplified: 6x + 4y. These two terms are unlike, so the expression cannot be simplified further.

Example 3 — Mixed Signs and Three Groups

Core
Simplify  5a − 2b + 3a − 4b + 1
Step 1 — Identify the three groups of like terms
a-terms: 5a, 3a  |  b-terms: −2b, −4b  |  constant: 1
There are three distinct pronumeral parts: a, b, and a constant. The negative signs belong to the terms they precede — −2b and −4b are negative b-terms.
Step 2 — Regroup using the commutative property
5a + 3a − 2b − 4b + 1
Bring like terms together. The signs travel with their terms — −4b stays negative when moved.
Step 3 — Collect each group
a-terms: 5a + 3a = 8a
b-terms: −2b − 4b = −6b
constant: 1
Add (or subtract) the coefficients within each group. −2 + (−4) = −6, so the b-terms combine to −6b.
Step 4 — Write the simplified expression
8a − 6b + 1
Three unlike terms remain. This is fully simplified — no further collection is possible.
✅ Simplified: 8a − 6b + 1

Example 4 — Multiplying and Dividing Terms

Core
Simplify:   (a)  3x × 4y    (b)  10ab5b
Part (a) — Multiply the coefficients
3 × 4 = 12
Multiply the numerical parts first. Use the commutative and associative properties to rearrange: 3 × x × 4 × y = (3 × 4) × (x × y).
Part (a) — Combine the pronumerals
x × y = xy   →   3x × 4y = 12xy
Letters placed side-by-side means multiply. Write alphabetically by convention. Unlike collecting like terms, multiplication combines unlike pronumerals into a single new term.
Part (b) — Write as a fraction and identify common factors
10ab5b   common factors: 5 and b
Identify what appears in both numerator and denominator. Here, 5 divides into 10, and b appears in both.
Part (b) — Cancel the common factors
10ab5b = 2a1 = 2a
10 ÷ 5 = 2; b ÷ b = 1. The denominator becomes 1, so it disappears. Cancelling a factor in algebra is identical to cancelling in numeric fractions.
✅ (a) 12xy    (b) 2a

Example 5 — Mixed Operations

Extension
Simplify  4m × 2n + 12m²n ÷ 4m
Step 1 — Apply order of operations: multiplication and division before addition
Evaluate 4m × 2n first, and 12m²n ÷ 4m first
BIDMAS: × and ÷ are performed before + and −. Treat each product/quotient as a single calculation before combining.
Step 2 — Simplify 4m × 2n
4m × 2n = 8mn
Multiply coefficients: 4 × 2 = 8. Combine pronumerals: m × n = mn. Result: 8mn.
Step 3 — Simplify 12m²n ÷ 4m
12m²n4m = 3mn
12 ÷ 4 = 3. m² ÷ m = m (one factor of m cancels). n remains. Result: 3mn.
Step 4 — Add the two results (they are like terms)
8mn + 3mn = 11mn
Both results have the same pronumeral part mn — they are like terms. Add the coefficients: 8 + 3 = 11.
✅ Simplified: 11mn. The key was applying order of operations before collecting like terms.

🔍 Error Detective — find the mistake

Each case has exactly one error. Read every line carefully, click the line you think is wrong, then click Reveal.
🔍 Case 1 — Simplifying an expression
Task: Simplify   3x + 2 + x

Click the line that contains the error:

Line 1:   Like terms: 3x and x (both have pronumeral x)
Line 2:   3x + x = 4x   (add coefficients: 3 + 1 = 4)
Line 3:   4x + 2 = 6x   (combine the remaining terms)
❌ The error is on Line 3

The working tries to add 4x and 2 — but these are unlike terms. 4x contains a pronumeral; 2 is a constant. They cannot be combined by addition.

  • Think of it as: 4 apples + 2 — you cannot add objects to a plain number.
  • The expression 4x + 2 is already fully simplified. It has two unlike terms and cannot be reduced further.
✅ Correct:   3x + 2 + x = 4x + 2

Error pattern: treating unlike terms (pronumeral term and constant) as like terms.

🔍 Case 2 — Multiplying algebraic terms
Task: Simplify   3a × 2a

Click the line that contains the error:

Line 1:   Multiply coefficients: 3 + 2 = 5
Line 2:   Multiply pronumerals: a × a = a²
Line 3:   Answer: 5a²
❌ The error is on Line 1

When multiplying terms, the coefficients are multiplied together — not added. The working used addition (3 + 2 = 5) instead of multiplication (3 × 2 = 6).

  • Adding coefficients is what you do when collecting like terms (e.g. 3a + 2a = 5a).
  • Multiplying terms is a different operation — the coefficients must be multiplied.
✅ Correct:
3a × 2a = (3 × 2) × (a × a) = 6a²

Error pattern: applying the addition rule for like terms to a multiplication operation.

📖 Word Problems — Newman's Framework

Each problem is broken into 5 Newman's steps. Work through the sentence frames before viewing the full solution.

Problem 1 — Stickers

1-step
Three friends each have x stickers. They combine their collections, then Sam finds 5 more stickers and adds those too. Write a simplified expression for the total number of stickers, then evaluate when x = 12.
① READ — What is the problem asking me to do?
I need to write an expression for the total stickers, then _____________ when x = _____.
② DEFINE — What does x represent?
Let x = the number of stickers each _____________ has.
③ WRITE — Build and simplify the expression
Total = x + x + x + 5 = ___x + ___
④ SOLVE — Substitute x = 12
3(___) + 5 = ___ + 5 = ___
⑤ ANSWER — Write a sentence
When each friend has 12 stickers, the total is _____ stickers.
✅ Full Solution
① Read: Write a simplified expression; evaluate when x = 12
② Define: x = number of stickers each friend has
③ Write: x + x + x + 5 = 3x + 5 (collected the x-terms)
④ Solve: 3(12) + 5 = 36 + 5 = 41
⑤ Answer: When each friend has 12 stickers, the total is 41 stickers.

Problem 2 — Garden Seedlings

2-step
A gardener plants n seedlings in each row. She plants 4 rows of tomatoes, then 2 rows of cucumbers, then 3 more rows of tomatoes in a second garden bed. Write a simplified expression for the total seedlings, then evaluate when n = 8.
① READ — How many separate plantings are there?
There are _____ separate plantings: _____ rows of tomatoes (split across two beds) and _____ rows of cucumbers.
② DEFINE
Let n = the number of _____________ in each row.
③ WRITE — Unsimplified, then simplify
Total = 4n + 2n + 3n = _____n
④ SOLVE — Substitute n = 8
9 × _____ = _____
⑤ ANSWER
When there are _____ seedlings per row, the total is _____ seedlings.
✅ Full Solution
① Read: 3 plantings — tomatoes (4 + 3 rows) and cucumbers (2 rows)
② Define: n = seedlings per row
③ Write: 4n + 2n + 3n = 9n (all like terms — collect the n-terms)
④ Solve: 9 × 8 = 72
⑤ Answer: When there are 8 seedlings per row, the total is 72 seedlings.

Note: Even though the tomatoes are in separate beds, the terms 4n and 3n are like terms and combine. The physical separation does not affect the algebra.

Problem 3 — Café Revenue

Multi-step
A café sells coffee for $c and cake for $d. In the morning they sell 5 coffees and 3 cakes. In the afternoon they sell 2 coffees and 3 cakes. Write a simplified expression for the total number sold of each item. Then find the total revenue when c = 4 and d = 5.
① READ — Two different items. What do I track separately?
I need to track _____________ separately from _____________.
② DEFINE
c = price of one _____________;   d = price of one _____________
③ WRITE — Total revenue expression (unsimplified)
Revenue = 5c + 3d + 2c + 3d
③ SIMPLIFY — Collect like terms
c-terms: 5c + 2c = _____c;   d-terms: 3d + 3d = _____d;   Total = _____c + _____d
④ SOLVE — Substitute c = 4 and d = 5
7(___) + 6(___) = _____ + _____ = $_____
⑤ ANSWER
The total revenue is $_____ when coffee costs $4 and cake costs $5.
✅ Full Solution
① Read: Track coffee sales and cake sales separately, then find total revenue
② Define: c = coffee price ($); d = cake price ($)
③ Write: 5c + 3d + 2c + 3d
③ Simplify: (5c + 2c) + (3d + 3d) = 7c + 6d
④ Solve: 7(4) + 6(5) = 28 + 30 = $58
⑤ Answer: The total revenue is $58 when coffee costs $4 and cake costs $5.

Foundation: Identify like terms, basic collection, simple multiplication.
Q1. Which pair of terms are like terms?
Like terms need exactly the same pronumeral part — same letters AND same powers. The coefficient (the number in front) does not matter for identification.
Q2. Simplify   2x + 5x
Both terms have x. Add only the coefficients: 2 + 5. The x stays unchanged.
Q3. Simplify   8a − 3a
8a − 3a: subtract the coefficients (8 − 3). The a stays as is.
Q4. Simplify   3 × 4m
3 × 4m = 3 × 4 × m. Multiply the numbers first. What is 3 × 4?
Core: Simplify expressions with addition, subtraction, multiplication, and simple algebraic fractions.
Q1. Simplify   4x + 3y − 2x + y
Identify two groups: x-terms (4x and −2x) and y-terms (3y and +y). Collect each group separately.
Q2. Simplify   3a × 4a
Multiply the coefficients together (3 × 4). Then multiply the pronumerals together (a × a). What is a × a?
Q3. Simplify   10ab5b
Cancel the numbers first: 10 ÷ 5. Then look for any pronumeral that appears in both numerator and denominator — cancel those too.
Q4. Simplify   5m + 2n × 3   (apply order of operations)
BIDMAS: multiplication before addition. Calculate 2n × 3 first, then consider what is left to add.
Extension: Division of terms with powers, mixed operations, pattern recognition, and error analysis.
Q1. Simplify   6a²b2ab
Cancel in three steps: (1) divide the coefficients, (2) cancel the a factors (how many are left?), (3) cancel the b factors.
Q2. Simplify   2x × 3y + 4xy
BIDMAS first: do 2x × 3y before adding. What do you get? Is the result a like term with 4xy?
Q3. Which expression is already fully simplified?
Check each expression: can any terms be collected? Can any multiplication be performed? If no operation remains, it is fully simplified.
