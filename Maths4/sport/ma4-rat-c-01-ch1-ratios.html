📘 Key Concept: RATIO

Definition

A ratio compares two or more quantities of the same unit. Written as a : b or a : b : c.

Example

In a cricket team of 11, if 6 are batters and 5 are bowlers, the ratio is 6 : 5.

Non-example

Speed (km/h) is NOT a ratio — it compares different units (km and hours). That's a rate.

Key facts

  • Order matters: 3:5 ≠ 5:3
  • Can be simplified by dividing by the HCF
  • Both quantities must be in the same unit
Worked Example 1 Simplifying a ratio — NRL possession
🏉 NRL

In a State of Origin game, NSW had 40 minutes of possession and QLD had 32 minutes. Write and simplify the ratio of NSW : QLD possession.

1
Write the ratio: NSW : QLD = 40 : 32
2
Find the HCF of 40 and 32: factors of 40 = {1,2,4,5,8,10,20,40}, factors of 32 = {1,2,4,8,16,32} → HCF = 8
3
Divide both parts by 8: 40 ÷ 8 = 5, 32 ÷ 8 = 4
✅ NSW : QLD = 5 : 4 (simplified)
Worked Example 2 Part as a fraction of the whole — cricket squad
🏏 Cricket

In a cricket squad, the ratio of batters to all-rounders to bowlers is 4 : 3 : 4. What fraction of the squad are all-rounders?

1
Find the total parts: 4 + 3 + 4 = 11 parts
2
All-rounders represent 3 parts out of the total 11 parts.
3
Fraction of all-rounders = 311
✅ All-rounders are 311 of the squad
Worked Example 3 Unitary method — sharing prize money (AFL)
🦘 AFL

Two AFL clubs split their facility grant in the ratio 3 : 5. The total grant is $240 000. How much does each club receive?

1
Total parts: 3 + 5 = 8 parts
2
Value of 1 part: $240 000 ÷ 8 = $30 000
3
Club A (3 parts): 3 × $30 000 = $90 000
4
Club B (5 parts): 5 × $30 000 = $150 000
5
Check: $90 000 + $150 000 = $240 000 ✓
✅ Club A receives $90 000, Club B receives $150 000
Worked Example 4 Scaling a ratio — basketball training
🏀 Basketball

A basketball training plan uses shooting drills to defensive drills in the ratio 2 : 3. If the team does 18 defensive drills, how many shooting drills do they do?

1
Ratio is shooting : defensive = 2 : 3. Defensive drills = 18.
2
3 parts → 18, so 1 part → 18 ÷ 3 = 6
3
Shooting drills = 2 × 6 = 12
✅ 12 shooting drills
🔍 Error Detective — spot the mistake

A student was asked to simplify 24 : 36 and divide $180 in that ratio. Find the error:

Question: Simplify 24 : 36, then divide $180 in that ratio. Student's work: Step 1: HCF of 24 and 36 = 6 Step 2: 24 ÷ 6 = 4, 36 ÷ 6 = 6 Step 3: Simplified ratio = 4 : 6 Step 4: Total parts = 4 + 6 = 10 Step 5: 1 part = $180 ÷ 10 = $18 Step 6: Share A = 4 × $18 = $72 Step 7: Share B = 6 × $18 = $108
Error in Step 1 and 2: The HCF of 24 and 36 is 12, not 6. Using 6 gives 4:6, which is not fully simplified. The correct simplification is 24÷12 : 36÷12 = 2 : 3. This then gives 1 part = $180 ÷ 5 = $36, Share A = $72, Share B = $108. Interestingly, the final dollar amounts happen to be the same here, but the simplified ratio is wrong — this won't always be harmless!

💬 Looking back: key ideas

  • A ratio compares quantities of the same unit — order matters.
  • Simplify by dividing both parts by their HCF.
  • To express a part as a fraction: parttotal parts
  • The unitary method: find the value of 1 part first, then scale.
  • Always check: parts should sum back to the original total. ✓
