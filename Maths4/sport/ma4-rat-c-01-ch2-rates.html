🏊 Swimming · 🏃 Athletics

🏊 Two swimmers, two distances

At the 2024 Paris Olympics, Ariarne Titmus swam 400 metres freestyle in 3 minutes 57 seconds (237 seconds). At the same meet, a 50 m sprint specialist swam 50 metres in 24 seconds.

Both athletes are elite swimmers — but who moved through the water faster?

💭 Could you compare these two swimmers using a simple ratio like 237 : 24? Why or why not? What extra step would you need to take?

The difference matters

⚖️ Ratio

Compares quantities of the same unit.

  • Goals : behinds (both in "shots")
  • Wins : losses (both games)
  • No unit in the simplified answer

⚡ Rate

Compares quantities of different units.

  • Speed: metres per second (m/s)
  • Scoring: points per game (pts/game)
  • The unit always appears in the answer
💭 Look at these: "15 goals in 3 games" and "15 goals out of 18 scoring shots" — which is a rate and which is a ratio? How do you know?

📘 Key Concept: RATE

Definition

A rate compares two quantities of different units. Written as "quantity per unit", e.g., 60 km/h or $2.50/kg.

Examples in sport

Ariarne Titmus: 1.69 m/s
NRL scoring: 18 pts/game
Cricket run rate: 6.5 runs/over

Non-example

Goals : behinds (6:4) — same type of thing, so it's a ratio.

Key facts

  • Always includes two different units
  • A "unit rate" has denominator of 1
  • Converting units changes the numbers, not the meaning
Worked Example 1 Simplifying a rate — cricket run rate
🏏 Cricket

Australia scored 312 runs in 50 overs in a One Day International. Write this as a simplified unit rate (runs per over).

1
Identify the rate: 312 runs in 50 overs
2
Divide both quantities: 312 ÷ 50 = 6.24
3
The unit rate is 6.24 runs per over
✅ Australia's run rate = 6.24 runs/over
Worked Example 2 Converting rate units — sprinting speed
🏃 Athletics

A sprinter runs 100 m in 10.5 seconds. Express this speed in km/h.

1
Start with the rate: 100 m in 10.5 s
2
Convert metres to kilometres: 100 m = 0.1 km
3
Convert seconds to hours: 10.5 s = 10.53600 h = 0.002917 h
4
Calculate km/h: 0.1 ÷ 0.002917 ≈ 34.3 km/h
Shortcut: To convert m/s to km/h, multiply by 3.6. So: 100÷10.5 ≈ 9.52 m/s × 3.6 ≈ 34.3 km/h ✓
✅ Speed ≈ 34.3 km/h
Worked Example 3 Best buy — sports nutrition
🏊 Swimming

A swimming club is buying protein powder. Brand A: 500 g for $22.00. Brand B: 1.2 kg for $49.20. Which is better value?

1
Brand A: $22.00 ÷ 500 g = $0.044 per gram (4.4 cents/g)
2
Brand B: Convert 1.2 kg = 1200 g. $49.20 ÷ 1200 g = $0.041 per gram (4.1 cents/g)
3
Compare: $0.041 < $0.044, so Brand B is cheaper per gram.
✅ Brand B is better value at 4.1 cents per gram
Key strategy: Always convert to the same unit before comparing. Here we converted kg → g so both were in g.

🔍 Error Detective — unit conversion trap

A student calculated an AFL player's scoring rate. Find and explain the error:

Question: An AFL player scores 156 points across a 22-game season. Express this as a unit rate. Student's work: Rate = 156 ÷ 22 = 7.09 Answer: The player scores 7.09 per game.
The answer is numerically correct but the unit is missing. A rate must always include its units. The correct answer is 7.09 points per game. Without "points per game", the answer is incomplete and could mean anything. In an exam, this would likely lose marks.

💬 Key ideas from Chapter 2

  • A rate compares two quantities with different units — the unit must always be stated.
  • A unit rate has a denominator of 1 (e.g., 8.5 runs per over).
  • To convert rate units: convert the top unit, then the bottom unit, separately.
  • Shortcut: m/s × 3.6 = km/h; km/h ÷ 3.6 = m/s.
  • For best buys: convert to the same unit, then compare price per unit.
