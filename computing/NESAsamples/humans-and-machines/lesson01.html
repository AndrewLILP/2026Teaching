Have Humans and Machines Become ONE?

Today you meet the provocation that will follow you through this whole unit, get a working definition of mechatronics, and use DART — Directed Activities Related to Text — to sort real mechatronic systems into the areas where they're changing lives.

How this lesson fits

This is the unit's opening lesson and its only job is to provoke, not resolve. Students should leave unsure whether they agree with the statement — that uncertainty is the engine for the Lesson 13 debate. The DART sorting activity below uses five real sources as directed texts: students read/watch first, then use what they found to extend an already-started categorisation, rather than starting from a blank page. This is a reconstruction-style DART, not a comprehension worksheet.

Put the statement up and take a show of hands before any teaching happens — agree, disagree, unsure. Record the split (a tally on the board is enough) and revisit it in Lesson 13. Watching their own thinking move over 13 lessons is part of the point.

"Humans and machines have become ONE."

Over this unit you'll research a real mechatronic or automated system of your choice, build an interactive historical timeline of it, and use everything you find as evidence for your own answer. You don't need to know what you think yet — you need to start noticing.

What is mechatronics?

Mechatronics blends mechanical engineering, electronics and computing into a single discipline for designing smart devices — anything that senses, decides and acts.

Poll questions

Discuss these as a class, or answer individually first and compare:

Open-ended discussion

  1. Can you provide other examples of areas where mechatronic systems may be used?
  2. In what areas do you think we need more robots and/or mechatronic solutions?
  3. In what areas do you think we do not need robots and/or mechatronic solutions?
  4. What are some of the benefits of automated systems?
  5. What are some of the risks with automated systems that need to be considered?
  6. How can developers ensure that automated systems are ethical?

Quick check
