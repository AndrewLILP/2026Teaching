NESA Sample Units

NESA publishes sample units alongside the Computing Technology 7–10 syllabus to illustrate how outcomes and content translate into real teaching and learning. This collection turns those official samples into interactive, accessible, lesson-by-lesson resources — organised here by focus area.

Mechatronic & Automated Systems

Mechatronics units

Three NESA sample units exploring sensors, actuators, automated systems and their history and applications.

How It Works: Investigating Inputs and Outputs

NESA Life Skills Year 9 sample unit. Sensors, actuators and effectors, the IPO model, a VEX VR build, and careers in mechatronics. 9 lessons · 50 minutes each.

Have Humans and Machines Become ONE?

NESA Stage 5 Sample Unit 3. Students research the historical evolution of mechatronic and automated systems and design an interactive timeline to present their findings.

Digital Wellbeing

Students design and build a microcontroller-based mechatronic device that supports a user's physical, emotional, social or cognitive wellbeing — a real-world problem of their own choosing.
