NESA Sample Unit · Software Development: Building Mechatronic and Automated Systems

How It Works: Investigating Inputs and Outputs

Students investigate the sensors, actuators and effectors used in everyday mechatronic and automated systems, compare human-controlled and automated machines, and document their thinking through diagrams, photographs and CAD. The unit closes with a hands-on build in VEX VR and a look at jobs and skills in mechatronics.

CT5-DPM-01 CT5-EVL-01 CT5-OPL-01 CT5-THI-01 CT5-DES-01

9 lessons · 50 minutes each · roughly one term

About this resource

This is a shorter, concept-first companion to the full 10-week Mechatronics unit elsewhere on this site. It follows NESA's own sample unit closely — sensors, actuators and effectors first, real-world investigation second, and a guided VEX VR build (Blocks → Python) as the practical centrepiece. Use it as a standalone intro unit, or as the front end of a longer mechatronics program.

The Design & Production Framework

Every lesson is tagged with the D&P phase it belongs to, so you always know whether you're investigating, planning, building or evaluating.

Identifying & Defining — Lessons 1–3 Researching & Planning — Lessons 4–6 Producing & Implementing — Lessons 7–8 Testing & Evaluating — Lesson 9
Identifying & Defining

Lessons 1–3: What is a mechatronic system?

Lesson 1 — What Is a Mechatronic System?

Sensors, actuators and effectors, introduced through everyday school, home and community examples, plus a paired role-play activity.

Lesson 2 — Inside the IPO Chain

A closer look at how signals travel through a system — input, process, output — using real devices as worked examples.

Lesson 3 — Human-Controlled or Automated?

Comparing systems that need a human trigger against systems that sense and act independently, and why designers choose one over the other.

Researching & Planning

Lessons 4–6: Investigate, choose, plan

Lesson 4 — Investigating a Real System

Students research one mechatronic system in depth and document it using diagrams, photos or simple CAD.

Lesson 5 — Choosing Our Problem

Selecting a real-world problem to solve, and weighing the social, ethical and legal implications of an automated solution.

Lesson 6 — Planning the Build

Block diagrams, component lists and non-functional requirements, ready to hand over to VEX VR.

Producing & Implementing

Lessons 7–8: Build it in VEX VR

Lesson 7 — Building in VEX VR: Blocks

First contact with the VEX VR simulator, building the sense → decide → act logic in Blocks.

Lesson 8 — Building in VEX VR: Python

Translating the Blocks solution into Python and refining the algorithm for reliability.

Testing & Evaluating

Lesson 9: Test, evaluate, look ahead

Lesson 9 — Testing, Evaluating & Careers in Mechatronics

Testing the built solution against its requirements, evaluating its strengths and limits, and exploring jobs and skills in mechatronics.

Platform

The Producing & Implementing lessons use VEX VR (vr.vex.com), moving from Blocks to Python so students experience both visual and text-based programming with the same underlying logic.
