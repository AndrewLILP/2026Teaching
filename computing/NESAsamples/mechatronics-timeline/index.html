NESA Sample Unit · Mechatronic & Automated Systems + Designing for User Experience

Have Humans and Machines Become ONE?

Students investigate and develop an interactive historical timeline exploring the evolution of mechatronic and automated systems — choosing a real case-study system, tracing its milestones, perceptions and impact, then using all of it as evidence in a class debate on whether humans and machines have truly become one.

CT5-SAF-01 CT5-DPM-01 CT5-EVL-01 CT5-THI-01 CT5-DES-01 CT5-OPL-01

13 lessons · 50 minutes each · approximately 11 hours total

How this unit fits

This is a standalone NESA sample unit, built to complement — not replace — the site's main Mechatronics focus-area pages. It's the sibling unit to Investigating Inputs and Outputs, sharing the same cyan accent family so all mechatronics units read as one family on the master hub, but it runs a UI/UX-heavy build (an interactive timeline, not a physical system) and is the only one of the three to spend a full lesson on flowcharting and requirements specification for a chosen real-world automated system rather than a built one.

Unit at a glance

Every lesson maps to one of the four Design & Production phases.

Identifying & Defining

Identifying & Defining

The provocation, the unit overview, and your first brainstorm of automation through history.

Platform and tool choice

Unlike the other two mechatronics units, this one has no fixed build platform — students choose their own authoring tool in Lesson 7 from six options (interactive website, VR walk-through, interactive presentation, video/infomercial, digital storytelling app, comic strip), kept deliberately open per NESA's own example list. The one fixed constraint is the case-study system shortlist in Lesson 3 — automatic door, smoke detector, anti-lock braking, robotic vacuum, or traffic light — chosen to keep each system's decision logic simple enough to flowchart cleanly in Lesson 6.
