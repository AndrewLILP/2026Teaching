Identifying & Defining

Worked example: flowcharting the door

Every system on the shortlist boils down to one core decision: a sensor checks something, and the system acts differently depending on the answer. Here's that logic for the automatic door.

Start

Sensor runs continuously, watching the doorway

↓

Person detected within range?

Yes ↓

Motor opens the door

No ↓

Door stays closed

After either branch, the sensor goes straight back to checking — this loop runs continuously, which is why a flowchart for an automated system is really a circle drawn as a line. Note the diamond is always a question with a Yes/No answer; the rectangles are always actions.

Identifying & Defining

Functional requirements

A functional requirement says what a system must do. It always has three parts: the system's purpose, the user cases it needs to handle, and test cases that prove it actually works.

Worked example — automatic door Purpose: To open and close a doorway automatically so people can pass through without using their hands, especially while carrying items or using a wheelchair. User cases: A customer carrying a loaded tray walks toward the door · A wheelchair user approaches from the side · Someone stands nearby without intending to enter.
InputExpected output
Person within sensor range, walking toward the doorDoor opens
Person standing still 3 m away, not approachingDoor remains closed
A shopping trolley passes through the sensor range with no person attachedDoor opens — the sensor can't tell a trolley from a person, which is a real limitation worth specifying
Power outageDoor defaults to manual push-open, as a safety requirement

Non-functional requirements

A non-functional requirement says how well a system must perform — not what it does, but the quality bar it has to clear.

Common categories

Reliability

The automatic door must not fail to open during a fire evacuation.

Accessibility

The door must meet disability access standards for opening width and timing.

Durability

The mechanism must handle tens of thousands of open/close cycles without failing.

Response time

The door must open within about one second of detecting someone approaching.

Write two non-functional requirements for your own system, picking from these categories or naming your own.
