Recap

Everything up to now has been Identifying, Defining, Researching and Planning. From this lesson on, you're in Producing and Implementing — you're building the real thing, using the structure plan from Lesson 9 as your map.

Producing & Implementing

From notes to entries

Your Lesson 3 research notes were written for you, to capture everything. A timeline entry is written for someone else, who needs almost none of that detail to get the point.

Before — research note "1931 — Stanley Works' photoelectric door. Installed at Wilcox's Pier Restaurant in Connecticut, this door opened when a photoelectric 'magic eye' detected someone approaching. Need it responded to: waitstaff carrying loaded trays had no free hands to open doors."
After — timeline entry "1931 — The first 'magic eye' door. A photoelectric beam let restaurant staff carry full trays through without ever touching a handle."

Notice what survived the edit: the date, the one concrete change, and the reason it mattered — compressed into something that reads in seconds. Every entry you build today should do the same three jobs, in roughly that order.

Producing & Implementing

Content checklist

Before you move past an entry, check it has… A clear date or era · One concrete thing that changed, in plain language · A reason it mattered, not just a fact sitting there · Consistent formatting with every other entry you've already built

If you've got a milestone that doesn't fit your linear scale from Lesson 9, build it as its own clearly labelled "historical echo" entry now, rather than leaving it for later.

Producing & Implementing

Build time

Work through your structure plan from Lesson 9, converting each planned page, stop, slide, scene, node or panel into a finished content entry.

Choose your task

Foundation · D–E

Three entries

Build at least three entries — the start, one from the middle, and the end of your timeline — each with a date, what changed, and why it mattered.

Core · C

Full set, consistent

Build entries for every milestone in your structure plan, consistently formatted, including your historical echo entry if you have one.

Extension · A–B

Weave it in

Do the Core task, then deliberately reference your Lesson 4 perception shift or Lesson 5 impact lens inside at least one entry's wording, rather than leaving them as separate facts no one connects.

Teacher tip

Resist letting teams build interactivity today — that's deliberately Lesson 11. Content that isn't finished by the end of today eats into next lesson's build time.

Checkpoint

Three quick questions before you go.

1. According to today's lesson, what should every timeline entry include?
2. Which is the best timeline entry, based on today's worked example?
3. What does today's teacher tip warn against starting too early?
