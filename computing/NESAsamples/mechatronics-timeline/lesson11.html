Recap

Lesson 10 was content. Today is everything that makes a timeline feel like more than a document — the clicking, moving, choosing, and the accessibility decisions you specified back in Lesson 8 but probably haven't built yet.

Producing & Implementing

Interactivity by tool

"Interactive" means something different depending on what you're building. Find your row.

ToolWhat "interactive" means for you today
Interactive websiteWorking clickable navigation between entries — not just one long scrolling page
VR walk-throughMovement between stops, plus a clickable hotspot at each one
Interactive presentationButtons or triggers that jump between slides — not just a linear "next" arrow
Video / infomercialClickable chapter markers or timestamps, or built-in pause-and-reflect prompts
Digital storytelling appBranching choices that actually lead somewhere different, not just back to the same page
Comic stripClickable panels or hotspots that reveal extra information on tap
Producing & Implementing

Polish checklist

Pull out your Lesson 8 design decisions — today is where they actually get built, not just written down.

Before you call it done, check… Every accessibility must-have from your Lesson 8 row is actually implemented, not just planned · Clickable elements look clearly different from plain content (affordance) · The same action looks and behaves the same way everywhere (consistency) · Something visibly happens the moment a user clicks or taps (feedback)

Teacher tip

"We'll add the alt text later" almost always means it never gets added. Build accessibility into each entry as you make it interactive, not as a separate pass afterward.

Core · C

Fully interactive

Add interactivity across your whole timeline, and implement every accessibility decision you specified in Lesson 8.

Extension · A–B

Test it on someone

Do the Core task, then hand your timeline to a peer with no explanation and watch them try to use it. Fix one real problem you saw them hit.

Checkpoint

Three quick questions before you go.

1. For a digital storytelling app, what does "interactive" mean today?
2. What should you check about your Lesson 8 accessibility decisions today?
3. Why does today's extension task ask you to test your timeline on a peer with no explanation?

Notebook entry

Project notebook — Producing & Implementing

  • If you tested on a peer, record what they got stuck on and what you changed. Sentence starter: "When [peer] tried our timeline, they got stuck on... so we..."
