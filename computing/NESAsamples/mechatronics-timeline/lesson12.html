Testing Against Our Criteria

Your timeline is built. Today you decide what "successful" actually means for it, get specific feedback from a peer against that standard, and make one real change because of it.

CT5-DPM-01 CT5-THI-01
Today's lesson — 50 minutes: Recap (5 min) · Define your success criteria (10 min) · Peer evaluation (15 min) · Rate and refine (10 min) · Checkpoint (5 min) · Notebook + CPD (5 min)
Testing & Evaluating

Recap

You've built content, interactivity, and accessibility into your timeline over Lessons 10 and 11. Today asks two simple-sounding questions that are actually hard: what would make this successful, and how successful is it really?

Testing & Evaluating

Define your success criteria

Before anyone can judge your timeline, you need to say what you're actually judging it against. Build on the four criteria from the brief — functionality, accessibility, usability, aesthetics — but make each one specific to your timeline, not generic.

We do — turning a generic criterion into a specific one

Generic: "The timeline should be accessible." Specific: "Every image has alt text, and someone using only a keyboard can reach every entry." The second version is something you — or a peer — can actually check.

Write one specific, checkable success criterion for each of the four areas.

Testing & Evaluating

Peer evaluation

Swap with another team. Use their success criteria (or the four general areas if they haven't finished theirs) and give specific feedback — not "looks good," but exactly what's working and what isn't.

Example — specific vs vague feedback Vague: "Pretty good, I liked it." Specific: "All the buttons worked except the one on the third entry — clicking it did nothing."

Testing & Evaluating

Rate and refine

Read the feedback you received, then answer NESA's actual question: how would your timeline and presentation be rated overall — not feature by feature, but as a whole?

Choose your task

Foundation · D–E

Read and respond

Read your peer feedback and write one sentence on whether you agree with each point.

Core · C

Rate overall

Give your timeline an overall success rating, with a paragraph justifying it using both your own criteria and the peer feedback you received.

Extension · A–B

Make the fix

Do the Core task, then actually make one genuine refinement based on the feedback, and explain exactly what changed and which criterion it improved.

Teacher tip

Push back gently on "we rate it 10/10, no changes needed" — every build has at least one thing worth refining, and finding it is the actual point of today.

Checkpoint

Three quick questions before you go.

1. What does today's lesson ask you to do before evaluating your timeline?
2. Which is the most useful peer feedback, based on today's lesson?
3. What does "rating the timeline overall," per today's lesson, actually mean?

Notebook entry

Project notebook — Testing & Evaluating

  • Record your overall success rating and your justification for it. Sentence starter: "Overall, our timeline is... because..."
  • Record the one refinement you made (or would make) based on peer feedback, and which criterion it improves. Sentence starter: "Based on feedback, we changed... which improved..."
