Kart World Racing

No gravity tricks here — just a kart, a track, and the clock. You'll build vehicle physics that actually feels like driving, checkpoints that time each lap honestly, and opponent karts that give a real race. Three sprints — the first builds the car and the track, the second adds timing and AI, the third finishes with multiple tracks, UI, and full race results.

Unity 6 C# NavMesh ShaderGraph
Stage 5 CPD E — Identifies D — Basic C — Sound B — Thorough A — Extensive

Your Mission

You're building a short, finishable racing game: a kart that accelerates, steers, and drifts convincingly; a track with checkpoints that can't be skipped or driven backwards through; and at least one opponent kart that follows the track without needing scripted waypoints memorised by hand. Sprint 1 gets the car and a first track working. Sprint 2 makes it a real race — honest lap timing and an opponent. Sprint 3 turns it into a finishable game with multiple tracks and clear results.

Before you start Sprint 1, make sure your Lessons 1–2 Game Design Document has pseudocode for at least your steering and acceleration system — Sprint 1 builds directly from it.

Sprints

Each sprint checkpoint requires all prior sprint behaviours to still work before you advance.

🛞 Sprint 1 · Build

Vehicle Physics & Track Collision

Acceleration, steering, and drift using Rigidbody forces, plus track boundary collision — pseudocode to C#, with checkpoint testing.

 🏁 Sprint 2 · Build

Lap Timing, Checkpoints & Opponent AI

Ordered checkpoints that can't be skipped, an honest lap timer, and a NavMesh-driven opponent kart. Iterative development in action.

 🏆 Sprint 3 · Build

Multiple Tracks, UI & Race Results

A second track, a HUD showing lap and position, race-end results screen, and full negative testing. Final playable build for assessment.
