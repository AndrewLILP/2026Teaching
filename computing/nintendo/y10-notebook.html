Studio Panel Pitch

Prepare a short pitch (aim for 3–5 minutes) presenting your whole World 3 output to a studio panel — your teacher, and possibly peers acting as executives deciding whether to "greenlight" your project.

  • 1Playable build: Your finished Galaxy or Kart game, demonstrated live — not just described.
  • 2e-Store demo: Browse, filter, add to cart, and check out live.
  • 3Amiibo product: Your printed figure (or photo, if not yet printed) and its e-Store listing.
  • 4One pseudocode-to-code moment: Pick a single system and show the pseudocode next to the real code that implements it.
  • 5One bug, honestly told: A real bug you hit, how you found it, and what fixed it. This is evidence of testing, not a confession.
  • 6One redesign: If you started this whole term again, what's the one thing you'd do differently, and why?
Why a bug, on purpose?

A pitch that only shows the polished, working result hides the exact evidence that proves you tested anything. Naming a real bug and its fix is stronger evidence of Testing & Evaluating than a flawless-looking demo that might just mean you never pushed it hard enough to find the cracks.

Check Your Understanding

2. Why self-assess against the actual CPD grade descriptors rather than just estimating "how good" the work feels overall?
3. Why present the finished work live to a studio panel, rather than simply submitting the project files?
