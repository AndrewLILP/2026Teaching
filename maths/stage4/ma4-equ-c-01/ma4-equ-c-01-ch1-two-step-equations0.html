Solve Linear Equations Up To 2 Steps

The Number Trick

Try this with a partner. Pick any number — don't tell anyone what it is.

1. Think of a number.
2. Double it.
3. Add 6.
4. Halve your answer.
5. Subtract your original number.

Whatever number you started with... you should have got 3.

Try it again with a completely different starting number. Same result?

If your starting number is the pronumeral x, the trick is really just the equation:

(2x + 6) ÷ 2 − x = 3

No matter what x is, the x-terms cancel out and you're left with 3. This chapter is all about equations like this — how to build them, and how to solve for an unknown number in up to 2 steps. By the end, you'll be able to prove exactly why this trick always works.

Key Vocabulary

Click each card to flip it.

Equation
Definition: a mathematical statement that says two expressions are equal, using an "=" sign.
Example: 2x + 5 = 20
Non-example: 2x + 5 (this is an expression — no "=" sign, so it can't be solved for a value).
Pronumeral
Definition: a letter used to represent an unknown or changing number.
Example: in 3x + 4 = 19, x is the pronumeral.
Non-example: the numbers 3, 4 and 19 are not pronumerals — they're known values.
Inverse Operation
Definition: the operation that "undoes" another operation.
Example: subtraction undoes addition; division undoes multiplication.
Non-example: squaring a number is not the inverse of adding to it.
Linear Equation
Definition: an equation where the pronumeral is only raised to the power of 1 (no squares, no roots).
Example: 2x + 3 = 4x
Non-example: x² = 9 is not linear — it's quadratic.

Goal: get x by itself. There are two things attached to x: "+4" and "×3". Undo them in reverse order — subtract first, then divide.
Step 1: subtract 4 from both sides.
3x + 4 − 4=19 − 4
3x=15
Step 2: divide both sides by 3.
3x ÷ 3=15 ÷ 3
x=5

2x + 5 = 20 — solve for x.

Goal: undo "+5" first, then undo "×2".
Step 1: subtract 5 from both sides.
2x + 5 − 5=20 − 5
2x=15
Step 2: divide both sides by 2.
2x ÷ 2=15 ÷ 2
x=7.5

Notice the answer isn't a whole number — that's fine. Not every equation has an integer solution.

2x + 3 = 4x — solve for x. This one has the pronumeral on both sides.

Goal: get all the x-terms on one side first. Subtract the smaller x-term (2x) from both sides so you don't end up with a negative x-term.
Step 1: subtract 2x from both sides.
2x + 3 − 2x=4x − 2x
3=2x
Step 2: divide both sides by 2.
3 ÷ 2=2x ÷ 2
x=1.5

Check: 2(1.5)+3 = 6, and 4(1.5) = 6. ✓ Both sides match.

Newman's Word Problem

Priya's phone plan costs $15 upfront plus $0.50 per text message. Last month her bill was $32. How many text messages did she send?

Stage 1 — Read

Read the problem carefully, then continue.

Stage 2 — Comprehend

What do you understand the problem is asking?

"The problem gives a fixed cost of ___ plus a cost of ___ per text. The total was ___. I need to find ___."

Stage 3 — Transform

Turn the words into an equation. Let n = number of texts.

"Upfront cost + (cost per text × number of texts) = total bill", so: 15 + 0.5n = ___

Stage 4 — Process

Solve your equation for n.

Stage 5 — Encode

Write your final answer in a full sentence.

15 + 0.5n = 32
0.5n = 17
n = 34

Priya sent 34 text messages.
