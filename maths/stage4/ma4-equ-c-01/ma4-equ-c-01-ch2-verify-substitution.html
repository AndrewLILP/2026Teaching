Solve and Verify Linear Equations by Substitution

Prove It

A plumber uses this formula to work out a callout price, where c = total cost and h = hours on the job:

c = 60 + 45h

She sends this invoice to a customer after a job:

Callout fee$60
Hours worked3
Rate per hour$45
Total charged$195

Before the customer pays, they want to prove the total is correct — not just trust it.
How could they check, using the formula?

Substitute h = 3 into the formula and see if it matches the total charged:

c = 60 + 45(3) = 60 + 135 = 195

It matches — the invoice is correct.
This is exactly what "verifying by substitution" means: plugging a value into a formula or equation to check a claim is true.
In this chapter you'll use this skill to check solutions, and to solve for an unknown value when you substitute into a formula.

Key Vocabulary

Click each card to flip it.

Substitution
Definition: replacing a pronumeral with a known value in an expression, equation or formula.
Example: substituting x = 4 into 3x − 2 gives 3(4) − 2.
Non-example: simplifying 3x + 2x to 5x is not substitution — no value was given for x.
Verify
Definition: to check that a claimed solution actually makes an equation true.
Example: substituting x = 5 into 2x − 1 = 9 and confirming both sides equal 9.
Non-example: guessing an answer without checking it is not verifying.
Solution
Definition: the value of the pronumeral that makes an equation true.
Example: x = 7 is the solution to 2x − 3 = 11.
Non-example: x = 5 is not a solution to 2x − 3 = 11, since 2(5) − 3 = 7, not 11.
Formula
Definition: an equation that shows the relationship between two or more quantities.
Example: v = u + at relates velocity, initial speed, acceleration and time.
Non-example: 3x + 5 is an expression, not a formula — it doesn't relate named quantities.
I Do — Worked Examples

Verify that x = 4 is a solution to 3x − 2 = 10.

Goal: substitute x = 4 into the left-hand side (LHS) and check it equals the right-hand side (RHS).
Step 1: substitute x = 4 into the LHS.
LHS=3(4) − 2
Step 2: simplify and compare to the RHS.
=12 − 2
=10 = RHS ✓

Since LHS = RHS, x = 4 is a correct solution.

Verify that x = 7.5 is a solution to 2x3 + 5 = 10.

Goal: substitute x = 7.5 into the LHS and simplify carefully — work out the fraction before adding.
Step 1: substitute x = 7.5 into the LHS.
LHS=2(7.5)3 + 5
Step 2: simplify and compare to the RHS.
=153 + 5
=5 + 5
=10 = RHS ✓

Since LHS = RHS, x = 7.5 is a correct solution.

Find the value of t in the formula v = u + at, given v = 10, u = 3, a = 2.

Goal: substitute the known values first, then solve the resulting 2-step equation for t.
Step 1: substitute v = 10, u = 3, a = 2.
10=3 + 2t
Step 2: subtract 3 from both sides, then divide by 2.
10 − 3=3 + 2t − 3
7=2t
7 ÷ 2=2t ÷ 2
3.5=t

Newman's Word Problem

A rectangular garden bed has a width of 3 m and a length of (2x − 1) m.
Its area is 39 m².
Find x.

Stage 1 — Read

Read the problem carefully, then continue.

Stage 2 — Comprehend

What do you understand the problem is asking?

"The garden bed has a width of ___ m and a length of ___. The area is ___ m². I need to find ___."

Stage 3 — Transform

Turn the words into an equation.
Remember: Area = length × width.

"Area = length × width", so: 3 × (2x − 1) = ___

Stage 4 — Process

Solve your equation for x.

Stage 5 — Encode

Write your final answer in a full sentence.

3(2x − 1) = 39
2x − 1 = 13
2x = 14
x = 7

The value of x is 7.
