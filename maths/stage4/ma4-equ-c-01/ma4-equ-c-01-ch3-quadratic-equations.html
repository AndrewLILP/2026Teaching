Solve Quadratic Equations

The Garden Bed Problem

A square vegetable garden bed needs to cover exactly 12 m² of area.
What's the side length?

? m × ? m
= 12 m²

Have a guess before you check.
Is the side length a whole number, or something in between?

We need a number that, when squared, gives 12.
3² = 9, which is too small.
4² = 16, which is too big.
So the side length is somewhere between 3 and 4 — exactly √12 m, which is about 3.46 m.

Here's the interesting part.
(−√12)² also equals 12.
So mathematically, x² = 12 actually has two solutions.
Only one of them makes sense as a real garden bed length.
You'll explore why in the Investigate tab.

Key Vocabulary

Click each card to flip it.

Quadratic Equation
Definition: an equation where the pronumeral is squared.
The highest power of x is 2.
Example: x² = 9
Non-example: 2x + 3 = 4x is not quadratic.
It's linear, since x is only raised to the power of 1.
Square Root
Definition: a number that, when multiplied by itself, gives the original number.
Example: the square roots of 25 are 5 and −5, since 5² = 25 and (−5)² = 25.
Non-example: −25 has no real square root.
No real number squares to a negative.
Exact Form
Definition: an answer written using a surd (√), rather than rounded as a decimal.
Example: x = ±√5 is exact.
x ≈ ±2.24 is a decimal approximation of the same value.
Non-example: rounding √5 to 2.2 loses accuracy, so it isn't exact.
Operation
Definition: a mathematical process applied to numbers, such as adding, multiplying, or squaring.
Example: squaring is the operation used to turn x into x².
Taking a square root reverses it.
Non-example: simply writing a number down is not an operation.
I Do — Worked Examples

x² = 9 — solve for x.

3 × 3 = 9
Goal: undo the "squared" by taking the square root of both sides.
Remember: both a positive and a negative number can square to give the same positive result.
Step 1: take the square root of both sides.
√(x²)=√9
Step 2: write both possible values.
x=±3

Check: 3² = 9.
Also check: (−3)² = 9.
Both work, so both are valid solutions.

x² = 5 — solve for x, in exact form and as a decimal approximation.

Goal: 5 is not a perfect square, so the answer will involve a surd.
Give the exact answer first, then round it.
Step 1: take the square root of both sides.
√(x²)=√5
x=±√5

This is the exact form of the answer.

Step 2: write the decimal approximation, correct to 2 decimal places.
x≈±2.24

Both forms describe the same two numbers — exact form is precise, and the decimal form is easier to picture.

Find the value of r in the formula A = π·r², given A = 10.

Goal: substitute the known value, then isolate r using inverse operations.
Since r is a real-world radius, only the positive root will make sense here.
Step 1: substitute A = 10.
10=π·r²
Step 2: divide both sides by π, then take the square root.
10 ÷ π=r²
√(10 ÷ π)=√(r²)
r≈1.78

Mathematically, r ≈ ±1.78.
A radius can't be negative, so only r ≈ 1.78 makes sense here.

Investigate — Why Two Solutions?

Two Squares, Same Answer

Look at these two squares side by side.
One has a side length of 3.
The other has a side length of −3.

3 × 3
3² = 9
−3 × −3
(−3)² = 9

A multiplying-by-itself pattern like this always gives a positive result, whether the starting number was positive or negative.
That's why every equation of the form x² = c (where c is positive) has two solutions.

Back to the Garden Bed

x² = 12 has two mathematical solutions: √12 and −√12.
Only √12 makes sense as the side length of a real garden bed.
A length can't be negative.

Newman's Word Problem

A square rug has an area of 20 m². What is the side length, correct to 2 decimal places?

Stage 1 — Read

Read the problem carefully.
Then continue.

Stage 2 — Comprehend

What do you understand the problem is asking?

"The rug is a square shape.
Its area is ___ m².
I need to find its ___."

Stage 3 — Transform

Turn the words into an equation.
Remember: Area of a square = side length squared.

"Side length squared = area", so: x² = ___

Stage 4 — Process

Solve your equation for x.
Give the positive value only, since a rug can't have a negative side length.

Stage 5 — Encode

Write your final answer in a full sentence.

x² = 20
x = ±√20
x ≈ ±4.47
Only the positive value makes sense for a real rug.

The rug's side length is about 4.47 m.
