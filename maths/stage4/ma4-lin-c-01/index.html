About this resource

No data collected No accounts NESA 2022 aligned
  • Privacy: nothing you type or click leaves this browser. Any saved progress uses your device's local storage only — it is never transmitted, and clearing your browser data will remove it.
  • Accessibility: built with dyslexia-informed typography (Nunito / Nunito Sans), a warm off-white background to reduce visual stress, colour-safe feedback (never colour alone), and a font toggle including Atkinson Hyperlegible.
  • Curriculum alignment: designed to build fluency in MA4-LIN-C-01 — Linear relationships, drawing on prerequisite Stage 4 skills in algebraic expressions, substitution, and solving simple linear equations — under the NSW Mathematics 7–10 Syllabus (NESA 2022).
  • How it was built: content and question design were AI-assisted, with all mathematics, pedagogy and error patterns reviewed and approved by a NSW secondary mathematics teacher.
Scope and known limitations

Designed for in-class or home use on a modern browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge); works best on screens 360px and wider. Worked examples and practice questions cover plotting points, generating linear rules from patterns, graphing and comparing relationships, and solving equations graphically — extension beyond this scope (e.g. gradient–intercept derivations at Stage 5 depth) is out of scope for this Stage 4 resource. Progress and journal entries use this device's local storage only, so they do not sync across devices or browsers. This is an early prototype currently under active testing with teachers and students, and is expected to change based on that feedback.
