Plot and Identify Points on the Cartesian Plane

Plotting and reading coordinates, including points that aren't whole numbers.

Key Vocabulary

Click a card to flip it and reveal the definition.

Cartesian plane
Definition: A flat surface formed by two number lines (a horizontal x-axis and a vertical y-axis) that cross at right angles, used to plot points.

Example: The grid used to graph y = 2x.
Non-example: A single number line only shows one direction — it isn't a Cartesian plane.
Coordinates
Definition: An ordered pair of numbers (x, y) that describes the exact position of a point on the Cartesian plane.

Example: (3, 4) means 3 units right, 4 units up.
Non-example: Just writing "3" doesn't describe a position — you need both numbers.
Point
Definition: An exact location on the Cartesian plane, marked with a dot and usually labelled with a letter.

Example: Point A at (2, 5).
Non-example: A whole shaded region is not a single point.
Origin
Definition: The point where the x-axis and y-axis cross, with coordinates (0, 0).

Example: Every axis measurement starts counting from the origin.
Non-example: (0, 3) is not the origin — only (0,0) is.
I Do — Worked Examples

Plotting and Reading Points

Plot the point (3, 2)

Step 1: Start at the origin (0, 0) — coloured blue on the grid.
Step 2: The x-coordinate is 3, so move 3 units to the right along the x-axis (coloured orange).
Step 3: The y-coordinate is 2, so move 2 units up (coloured green).
Step 4: Mark and label the point (3, 2). ✅

Plot and label P13, −2) and Q(−3.5, 0)

Step 1: For P, the x-coordinate is 13 — a small step just past 0, about one third of the way to 1.
Step 2: The y-coordinate of P is −2, so move down 2 units. Mark P.
Step 3: For Q, the x-coordinate is −3.5 — halfway between −3 and −4.
Step 4: The y-coordinate of Q is 0, so Q sits exactly on the x-axis. Mark and label Q.

Reading a diagram: point A, point Z, and a described point

Step 1: Point A sits 2 units right and 1.5 units up → A(2, 1.5).
Step 2: Point Z lies on the x-axis directly above point C — "directly above" means the x-coordinate matches C's x-coordinate, and the y-coordinate must be 0 (on the axis).
Step 3: If C is at (−2.5, −1.5), then Z is at (−2.5, 0).
Step 4: The point 2½ units left of the origin is 2½ units in the negative x-direction with no vertical movement → (−2½, 0).

Newman's Word Problem

A park's coordinate map has the entrance gate at the origin (0, 0). The picnic shelter is 3 units east and 2 units north of the gate. The lookout tower is 2 units west and 4 units south of the gate. What are the coordinates of the picnic shelter and the lookout tower?

Read the problem carefully. What is it asking you to find?
