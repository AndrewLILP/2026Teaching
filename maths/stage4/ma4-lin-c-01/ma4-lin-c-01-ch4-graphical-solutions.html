Key Vocabulary

Click a card to flip it and reveal the definition.

Point of Intersection
Definition: The single point where two lines cross — it satisfies both equations at once.
Example: y = 2x + 1 and y = −x + 4 cross at (1, 3).
Non-example: Parallel lines never have a point of intersection.
Solution
Definition: The value that makes an equation true. On a graph, it's the x-coordinate where the line reaches the target y-value.
Example: For 2x+3=11, the solution is x=4.
Non-example: Writing "y=11" isn't the solution — you need the x-value.
Verify
Definition: To check a point satisfies an equation by substituting its coordinates and confirming both sides are equal.
Example: Verify (2,5) satisfies y=2x+1: 2(2)+1=5 ✓
Non-example: Just glancing at a graph without substituting isn't a full verification.
Linear Equation
Definition: An equation that forms a straight line when graphed.
Example: 2x + 3 = 11 comes from the linear rule y = 2x + 3.
Non-example: x² = 9 is not linear — its graph is curved.
I Do — Worked Examples

Solving Graphically

Verify that (2, 5) and (−1, −1) satisfy y = 2x + 1

Step 1: Substitute (2, 5): 2(2)+1 = 5. This matches the y-value, so (2, 5) satisfies the equation. ✓
Step 2: Substitute (−1, −1): 2(−1)+1 = −1. This also matches, so (−1, −1) satisfies the equation too. ✓
Step 3: Graph y = 2x + 1 and mark both points.
Step 4: Both points sit exactly on the line — this confirms both algebraically and visually that they satisfy the equation.

Use the graph of y = 2x + 3 to solve 2x + 3 = 11

Step 1: Graph y = 2x + 3.
Step 2: Draw a horizontal guide line at y = 11 to find where it meets the graph.
Step 3: Read off the x-coordinate at that intersection point — drop a line straight down to the x-axis.
Step 4: The solution is x = 4 (check: 2(4)+3 = 11 ✓).

Graph y = 2x + 1 and y = −x + 4 — find the point of intersection

Step 1: Graph y = 2x + 1 (gradient 2, intercept 1).
Step 2: Graph y = −x + 4 (gradient −1, intercept 4) on the same axes.
Step 3: The lines cross at (1, 3) — this is the point of intersection.
Step 4: Verify: for y=2x+1, 2(1)+1=3 ✓. For y=−x+4, −1+4=3 ✓. The point satisfies both equations.

Newman's Word Problem

Plan A costs $20 plus $0.10 per minute. Plan B costs $15 plus $0.15 per minute. At how many minutes do the two plans cost the same? Use a graph to find your answer.

Stage 1 — Read

Read the problem carefully. What is it asking you to find?

Stage 2 — Comprehend
